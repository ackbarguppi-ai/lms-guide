Corporate learning is continuously evolving, but what's certain is that learning management systems (LMSs) are the preferred technology, with [82% of organizations using it](https://research.com/careers/training-industry-statistics#:~:text=Only%2043%25%20of%20organizations%20use,to%2071%25%20of%20small%20companies.). It's a big step forward for companies with many employees working remotely from various parts of the globe.

Learning is much easier with the help of LMSs as they make e-learning courses available and accessible for all employees. The challenge is ensuring that learning objectives are met and that learners reach the skill levels necessary to perform well in their roles.

With learners having unprecedented autonomy, LMS assessments must be truly indicative of the levels of mastery achieved through the e-learning courses. Furthermore, L&D professionals depend on these assessments to demonstrate the effectiveness of every training program they deploy.

## Your platform offers many ways to create LMS assessments

Assessments are necessary but generally don't make for crowd-pleasers. Many adult learners still think of their sometimes stressful formative years whenever they come across the words "quiz," "test," or "multiple choice." Furthermore, instructional designers need to ensure that the online assessment results are accurate and accurately measure learner progress.

This is where a good LMS comes in handy, as it has many options to cater to learners' needs and make the evaluation process engaging and fun. The many types of LMS assessments, as well as the possibility to customize them, are essential for the success of corporate training programs.

## Best practices for creating LMS assessments

Online evaluations come with several challenges and there are several pitfalls instructors need to avoid. Here are some helpful tips about creating LMS assessments:

### Allow multiple attempts

The point of the training is for them to remember and apply the information, not to get it right on the first try. When the first answer is incorrect, the learner has to rethink the question and revisit the information, leading to a better understanding.

### Use the automated grading option

Automated grading eases the instructor's workload. Adult learners want autonomy — to feel like they are in charge of their learning. When the assessments are auto-grated, the results are immediate, thus keeping learners engaged with the content.

### Enrich with gamification

Learners can earn points and badges for completing assessments. The gamification of training motivates them to pass each assessment, and they're also rewarded automatically for their good results or attempts.

### Make use of learning analytics

LMS assessment results offer valuable insight into the effectiveness of training. They also hold the key to identifying knowledge gaps and content areas that are either too difficult or too easy. By employing the platform's learning analytics features, you can get comprehensive reports to showcase a specific program's strong and weak points.

### Use competency-based training

Corporate training and development focuses on building skills and competencies that employees need in the workplace. Ideally, your platform has a competency-based learning feature that you can use to ensure every e-learning course is built to evaluate clear and specific competencies.

## 9 Types of assessments you can create in your LMS

There are several types of assessments you can create in your LMS. The one you choose has to fit learners and the content of the online course. The format of your e-learning course will primarily determine which types of assessments will be suitable. Depending on the structure and subject of your course, you might want to include one or a mix of these assessments:

### 1\. Quizzes

Quizzes allow learners to answer a set of questions. There are many question types that you can add to a quiz, including the common true or false, multiple choice, and fill-in-the-blanks. You also have freeform, matching and even hotspot question options if you need something more advanced.

Even better, instructors can add questions from question banks, which are collections of questions that instructors can create and store for repeated uses.

Quizzes can also be graded automatically, which means that instructors just assign correct and wrong answers to each question and don't have to worry about manual grading. Learners get instant results and can try multiple times.

### 2\. Offline

Offline assessments don't require online submissions. It can be anything from reading a book, a case study, or working on a project.

The learners can let the instructor know when they are done with the task by marking it as "completed." This will remove it from the learner's to-do list. Subsequently, the instructor will grade each learner based on the results of the assigned offline work.

### 3\. Discussion

Setting up a discussion assessment encourages learners to participate by answering a discussion thread started by the instructor. The best part is that they can earn points and even work towards achieving mastery of a competency.

This way, you can avoid copy-paste interactions where most participants give the same answers to a question.

### 4\. Team

This type of assessment asks groups of learners to work on joint submissions. The instructor splits the learners into teams, each with a private group. This space can be used for collaboration of their submissions.

Once the team task is completed, the instructor can grade each learner based on participation and the end result. Team assessments are great tools for fostering collaboration and social learning.

### 5\. Survey

A survey assessment gives the learner a set of questions that can be either multiple-choice or free-form. Post-training surveys are often the most popular, but you can use surveys to conduct training needs assessments and check learner engagement while they're taking the course.

### 6\. Dropbox

This is an excellent medium for submitting lengthier files (like PDFs) and for building learning portfolios. These are important for more complex competencies (such as project management, for example) and also allow for collaboration and feedback.

For example, if the training program is aimed at building online marketing skills, the learners may be asked to build a campaign and submit it for review.

### 7\. Debate

Debate assessments might not seem like the first choice regarding corporate learning. However, when the training is about developing skills such as critical analysis or out-of-the-box thinking, this assessment is perfect.

Allowing the learners to add arguments for or against a statement is the best way to get a conversation going and foster collaboration and constructive feedback.

### 8\. H5P

H5P assessments are great when you need to make the evaluation process more engaging and when there are additional resources (such as videos). Apart from the "wow" factor of adding media content, these types of assessments are highly customizable.

### 9\. Peer-reviewed

Social learning is essential to training success. For our CYPHER Learning platform for Business, we teamed up with [Turnitin](https://www.turnitin.com/) — a cloud-based platform that performs originality checking, online grading, and peer review. It is a real-time-saver for instructors, providing varied and relevant feedback to learners.

### Bonus assessment options

- **LTI** stands for [Learning Tools Interoperability](https://www.imsglobal.org/activity/learning-tools-interoperability). It allows for the integration of other learning tools with the LMS while deploying and assessing training programs. You can use external resources such as courses, videos, audio content, maps, assessments, badges, etc.
- **SCORM** stands for [Shareable Content Object Reference Model](https://scorm.com/scorm-explained/one-minute-scorm-overview/). If assessments meet the SCORM standard for LMSs, they can be shared on any platform. Any assessment can be a SCORM assessment, and they work like third-party content you create with various other tools – like Adobe Captivate or PowerPoint.

## Glossary of terms

**Assessment builder:** A software tool that allows instructors and organizations to create, manage, and deliver online exams, quizzes, self-assessments, surveys, and questionnaires within a learning platform.

**Online assessment builder:** A digital solution used to design and administer assessments online, enabling automated grading, feedback, and performance tracking.

**Online exam builder:** A feature that enables the creation of structured, timed exams with multiple question types, grading rules, and attempt limits.

**LMS assessments:** Assessments created and delivered within a learning management system (LMS), allowing results to connect directly with learner profiles, progress tracking, and reporting.

**LMS assessment tools:** Built-in LMS features used to create, distribute, grade, and analyze assessments, eliminating the need for external testing platforms.

**Digital assessment platforms:** Standalone tools focused solely on testing and evaluation, often lacking integrated course delivery, learner management, or long-term tracking.

**Question types:** The different formats supported in assessments, such as multiple-choice, short answer, matching, media-based, or open-ended questions.

**Question banks:** Reusable collections of questions that can be randomized or reused across multiple assessments to ensure variety and consistency.

**Randomized questions:** A feature that delivers questions in a random order or selects random questions from a pool to reduce memorization and cheating.

**Gradebook:** A centralized area within an LMS where assessment scores, pass/fail status, and learner performance data are collected and managed.

**Assessment analytics:** Data and reports that show learner performance, completion rates, scores, time spent, and trends across assessments.

**Skills builder assessment:** An assessment designed to measure and validate specific skills or competencies, commonly used in corporate training and upskilling programs.

**Compliance assessments:** Formal assessments used to verify that learners have completed mandatory training and met regulatory or organizational requirements.

**Certificates:** Digital credentials awarded to learners after successfully completing assessments or certification programs.

**SCORM assessments:** Assessments delivered through SCORM packages whose scores and completion data are tracked and stored within an LMS.

**SCORM score integration:** The ability of an LMS to collect and display scores, completion status, and pass/fail results from SCORM-based assessments.

**AI-powered assessment creation:** The use of artificial intelligence to generate questions, feedback, or assessment content based on learning objectives or input prompts.

**AI-generated feedback:** Automated, contextual feedback provided to learners based on their answers, helping improve learning outcomes at scale.

**Conditional logic:** Rules that adapt assessment behavior based on learner responses, such as showing different questions or feedback paths.

**Personalization variables:** Dynamic elements (eg learner name, score, or course data) used to personalize questions, feedback, or assessment summaries.

**Assessment attempts:** The number of times a learner is allowed to take or retake an assessment.

**Server-side time tracking:** A security feature that measures assessment time on the server to prevent manipulation or cheating.

**Auto-recovery:** A feature that allows learners to resume an assessment without losing answers if they temporarily exit or lose connection.

## FAQs

### Standalone digital assessment platforms vs LMS assessment tools: What’s the difference?+

When comparing digital assessment platforms vs LMS, the main difference is how assessments connect to the rest of the learning experience. Standalone digital assessment platforms focus mainly on testing and evaluation and often need separate tools for courses, users, reporting, and certifications.

LMS assessment tools are built directly into a learning management system. This allows assessments to link seamlessly with courses, learner profiles, analytics, certifications, and compliance tracking, making them better suited for scalable training and education programs.

## Glossary of terms

**Interactive video:** A type of video content that allows learners to engage through clickable elements like quizzes, buttons, links, and branching scenarios directly within the video. Drives engagement, knowledge retention, and self-paced exploration.

**Hotspots:** Clickable areas on a video that reveal additional content, links, or trigger actions. Ideal for adding tooltips, onboarding guides, or compliance disclaimers.

**Overlay:** Content (text, images, buttons) that appears over the video to enhance context, prompt decisions, or direct learner actions—without interrupting playback.

**Branching scenarios:** Non-linear paths within a video where learner choices determine what happens next. Useful for simulations, role-play training, or compliance decision trees.

**Time-triggered interactions:** Events (eg pop-ups, questions, feedback) that appear at precise moments in the video timeline to reinforce learning or prompt action.

**Quizzes in video:** Built-in knowledge checks embedded inside the video to assess understanding, measure completion, and drive compliance tracking.

**Video chapters:** Sections of a video that allow learners to skip to relevant content easily. Enables flexible learning and faster navigation, especially in longer training sessions.

**Call-to-action (CTA):** Prompts like “Next Lesson,” “Download Guide,” or “Book a Call,” integrated into the video to guide learners toward the next step or action.

**Interactive transcript:** A synchronized text version of the video that learners can click to jump to specific parts. Enhances accessibility and supports skimming.

**Video progress tracking:** LearnWorlds tracks how much of a video a learner watches and when they interact, enabling detailed reporting for compliance and engagement.

**Mobile-optimized playback:** Ensures all video interactions work on smartphones and tablets, crucial for frontline or remote teams.

**SCORM-compatible videos:** LearnWorlds supports importing SCORM packages into video-based courses, maintaining progress tracking and completion logic.

**Embedded forms:** Interactive surveys or feedback forms displayed during or after the video to collect insights, confirmations, or training satisfaction data.

**Knowledge checks:** Short assessments embedded within videos to validate understanding before learners progress—best used for compliance and role-based certifications.

**Adaptive pathways:** Tailors video journeys based on user role, prior answers, or learning objectives, allowing for personalized internal training.

# Quizzes & assessments for your LMS

![](https://framerusercontent.com/images/WfvcZWvXQBFYQgbSSoNYbm5JX8.jpg?width=2035&height=2035)

Words by

Kaine Shutler

![](https://framerusercontent.com/images/Ai1289pAPBlBsHAFutMRAF5WdNQ.jpeg?width=2070&height=1380)

Quizzes and assessments are popular LMS features and are a great way to check your students' knowledge, keep them engaged throughout the course content, and help them learn new material faster. For some businesses and organizations, quizzes are instrumental to success, like drilling driving test questions or preparing for LSATs.

A good quiz tests different levels of learning, from simple comprehension and memorization of facts to analysis and critical thinking. To achieve this goal, you will need to create different questions for your students; the most frequently used ones are single choice, multiple choice, sorting choice, fill in the blank, assessment (survey), and essay (open answer).

At Plume, we offer infinitely scalable and customizable solutions for quizzes to ensure your learners get the best user experience possible and your website outshines the competition. Here is a checklist of tried and tested solutions that will make your quizzing campaign an even bigger success:

1. **Check at different levels** \- comprehension, analysis, and critical thinking. Various question types will help here, from the world’s favourite multiple choice question to something as advanced as H5P hotspots, flashcards, and interactive videos.

2. Keep the focus on the content with inline content checks. Keep these casual, don’t worry about assessing any knowledge at this point, you can just drop a joke there or a funny gif - the goal is to check if the learner is not zoning out or dozing off.

3. Provide feedback. One of the most exciting parts of the e-learning process is when you see your score - this is just how brains are wired - we’re much better at anything we do if it is somehow measured. With online technology available today, your students no longer need to wait for the test to be checked by the teacher manually; they can see the results in real time.

4. Challenge bank allows your students to record gaps in knowledge and double down on them. The feature enables your student to create a list of questions that they answered incorrectly and practice them once they’ve completed the test.

5. Acknowledge accomplishments. A sense of achievement and instant gratification is a well-known trick that keeps people hooked on apps. Rewarding your students for their efforts and acknowledging their achievements is a sure way to customer retention.

6. Prevent cheating. One way to do that is to create a question bank and randomize questions every time a student attempts a quiz. You can also take it one level up and introduce timings for each question. This will require your students to act fast and will discourage them from using google.

7. Practice with Drills. Repetition is an important element of e-learning, and one way to repeat content is through quizzes. In this article on brain science, Art Kohn argues that doing things seven times (more or less) helps learners retain the information. That’s why drilling is still, to this day, one of the most effective teaching methods.

## About This Feature

In order to view your Quiz and Survey results, you can run an export! This will send you an email with a link to download a CSV file where your results are structured for easy reading in your favorite spreadsheet editor.

> Thinkific Analytics: Engagement Dashboards allow you to see and analyze quiz and survey results directly in Thihnkific. Effortlessly access and analyze comprehensive reports on quiz and survey performance to better understand your student’s engagement with your materials.
>
> Make data-driven decisions with comprehensive information to empower you to evaluate quiz and survey effectiveness, optimize them, and enhance the learning experience for your students.
>
> Learn more here: [Thinkific Analytics: Engagement Dashboards](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/15795863469463).

You can easily filter for your desired Quizzes and Surveys by course and export one or more results in just a few clicks!

## How to Access Your Quiz & Survey Page

The **Quiz and Survey** section on the left sidebar of the **Admin Panel** lets you export the data from students' participation in quizzes and surveys.

To access this section:

1. From your Admin Dashboard, select **Users**
2. Select **Quiz and Survey**

## Finding the Quiz or Survey You Want

Use the sorting and filtering options to find one or many of the Quiz & Survey lessons that you would like to receive a report on. You can filter your results to include only lessons in a specific course. You can also sort the lessons in the results table by the course it belongs to, its type, the Quiz/Survey name or even the # of results and when the lesson was last completed by a student.

## Selecting a Date Range

You can also use the "Completion Date" date picker to customize the results you will receive when you begin your export.  This date picker affects the "# of results" column as well as the data that you will receive in your export.

> By default, your results will display your last month of data. If you don't see all results expected, make sure to adjust your date range before exporting.

This is useful if you only want to see submissions that your students have made in the last week or two without having to wade through all of your historical data.

## Export Your Results

Once you have set the timeframe that you want to look at, you can select the Quiz/Surveys that you want to export.

You can export results for a single Quiz/Survey by selecting the inline **Export** button on the right side of the table.

You can also export multiple Quiz/Surveys at the same time by selecting the checkbox at the top of the table or by checking the surveys and quizzes you want to export. Once selected, click on the **Export** button to exports results for all options

Once you have activated your exports we will get to work preparing the data and will send you an email with the results in a CSV format.

## Important Considerations

If the number of results for a quiz or survey is zero (i.e. there may not be results for a certain time period), an email will not be generated if an export is attempted. Please note that Admins attempting an export will see a message to expect an email with the CSV but an email will not be sent.

## About This Feature

It's been proven that testing your knowledge immediately after learning a new concept can help improve your ability to recall the information at a later time. This is important if you want to ensure that your students are receiving lasting value from your courses.

Quiz lessons will allow your students to test their knowledge throughout your course, while also providing you with the ability to export and review the results later!

## How It Works

Quiz lessons within Thinkific allow you to create multiple-choice questions for your students. If you choose to, you can also include multiple correct answers within a single question.

Question types:

- **One answer** \- Allows the student to select one option as an answer
- **One or more answers** \- Allows the student to select several correct answers and they must select all correct answers to receive a passing mark

To make your questions and answers engaging, you can also use the WYSIWYG text editor to insert a video, embed an image, or audio file within the questions and answers themselves. See [Combine Videos and Text in the Same Lesson](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030374854) for more tips on including mixed content within lessons.

We generally suggest including a quiz lesson at the end of a chapter, which will test students on the key takeaways within that chapter. If you want your students to complete the quiz before moving on to the next chapter within your course, you can set it as a [prerequisite lesson](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030741153).

## Important Considerations

- If you're more comfortable with filling out and uploading a spreadsheet that contains your questions and answers, this is an option! See [Importing Quiz Questions](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030373894) for more details on how.
- Once your students have completed your quiz, you have the ability to export their results. For steps on how to do this, see [Quiz & Survey Export](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030370334).

## How to Create a Quiz Lesson

1. From your Admin Dashboard, select **Products**
2. Select **Courses**
3. Click on a specific **course** in the [Course Overview](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030719613) to open the [Course Builder](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030371754)
4. From the **Curriculum** tab, select the **Add Lesson** button under your desired chapter on the left-hand side
5. Select **Quiz** from the lesson types displayed
6. Enter a title for your quiz

You will now need to add questions to your quiz. You can add questions either by using a Quiz AI generator, adding questions manually, or by importing them using a .xlsx file.

### How to Add Questions Using AI Quiz Generator

> This feature is available on the [Basic](https://www.thinkific.com/pricing/) plan and above.

Simplify quiz creation by utilizing the power of AI! The AI Quiz Generator allows you to quickly create a series of multiple-choice questions and answers based on existing text, PDF, and video lessons inside the chapter. _Note_ that videos must have [closed captions](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030375274) to be used in quiz generation. To create questions using AI Quiz Generator:

1. Open the **Create with AI** menu by clicking on the arrow
2. Select the **number of questions** you want to create
3. Select **Generate Questions**

_The AI Generator will create questions based on the information in **text, PDF, and video lessons** in **the same chapter** that the quiz is in. Videos in video lessons must have_ [_closed captions_](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030375274) _to be used in quiz generation._

_![](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/article_attachments/20302551661335)_

4. Once the system has generated the desired number of questions, review the questions and answers.

_The answers that the AI Generator determined to be correct will be marked as **(Correct)**._
5. If you are satisfied with the generated questions, select **Accept and add to the quiz**. Alternatively, select **Regenerate questions** to get a new set of questions and answers.

![](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/article_attachments/20302573505815)
6. Once the questions are added, you can edit, rearrange, or delete them by following the instructions below.

#### Important Considerations

- The AI Quiz generator is available on all paid plans (Basic and above).
- The AI Quiz Generator only uses information found in existing text, PDF, and video lessons within the same chapter where the quiz lesson is located. Videos in video lessons must have [closed captions](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030375274) to be used in quiz generation.


> Note that some creators might not have the ability to use the videos in AI Quiz Generation. We are working on bringing this functionality to all qualified creators soon.

- If your video lessons [have text added to them](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038741914-How-to-Add-Text-to-Your-Video-Lesson), the AI Quiz Generator will not use this text when generating questions.
- The AI Quiz Generator does **not** use information from videos, images, or external content embedded into a text lesson.
- The generated questions and answers will be based on the first 8,000-10,000 words found in the text, PDF, and video lessons in the chapter.
- To create AI-generated quiz questions from a PDF, the text in the PDF must be in a format that the AI can read and understand. This means the text should be **actual, selectable text** rather than images of text. If the text in the PDF is an image (like a photo or scan of a document), the AI cannot process it directly to create questions.
- The AI Quiz Generator can be used on new and existing quiz lessons.
- The generated questions will have three options and one correct answer. If you would like to add more choices or mark more than one answer as correct, you can do it by editing the questions once they are created.
- Once questions are created, you can edit, rearrange, and delete them as required.

5. Following steps 3 and 4, continue to add multiple choices for the answer

_If you need to create an additional choice, you can select the **add choice** button below the last answer. You can also select the **trash** icon in the right-hand corner of an answer if you want to delete it._

This explanation will be displayed regardless of whether they get the question right or wrong. However, if a_ [_passing grade is required for the quiz_](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360048237914) _, you have the option to enable or disable the display of question explanations under Lesson Settings._

## Setting a Passing Grade Requirement

If you want to ensure students are acing your quizzes, you can choose to require a passing grade!

When this is enabled, students will not be able to reach 100% completion of your course unless they have successfully passed all quizzes where a passing grade is required.

For further info and for steps on how to enable this, see: [Requiring a Passing Grade within a Quiz](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360048237914).

If you're not concerned about students passing your quiz, but you want to ensure that they take it, you can choose to set the quiz lesson as a prerequisite. A [prerequisite lesson](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030741153) will need to be completed before they can move on to the next chapter within your course.

## Randomized Question Bank

We also offer the ability to have a [Randomized Question Bank](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030740233) for your quiz lessons!

You can add as many questions as you want within a specific quiz "bank", then select how many questions should appear when a student takes the quiz. The system will then randomly generate a unique quiz for each student. [Read more about Randomized Question Banks.](https://support.thinkific.com/hc/en-us/articles/360030740233)
