## Scoring: Experts Persona

**Criteria** | **Thinkific** | **Kajabi** | **LearnWorlds** | **Teachable** | **Podia** | **Kartra**
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
1. Ease of Use & Speed to Launch | 4 | 5 | 3 | 5 | 5 | 3
2. Built-in Sales & Payment Processing | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4
3. Total Cost of Ownership | 4 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 3
4. All-in-One vs Best-of-Breed | 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 | 5 | 5
5. Course Delivery & Student Experience | 4 | 3 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 2
6. Marketing & Audience Growth Tools | 3 | 5 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 5
7. Scalability & Growth Path | 5 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 2 | 3
8. Community & Engagement Features | 5 | 3 | 4 | 2 | 3 | 2
9. Brand Control & Professional Appearance | 4 | 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 | 3
10. Support & Reliability | 4 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 3

### Scoring Rationale — Experts

**Thinkific:** Strong across the board. Excellent commerce (5), community/memberships (5), and scalability (5). Slightly behind Kajabi on ease of use and marketing tools because Kajabi bundles email marketing and funnels natively. Very strong growth path from free plan to Plus.

**Kajabi:** The "all-in-one" champion for experts. Best ease of use (5), marketing (5), and integrated experience (5). Weaker on course delivery sophistication, community depth, and scalability for larger operations. Higher price point hurts TCO (3).

**LearnWorlds:** Best course delivery experience (5) with interactive video. Strongest brand/white-label control (5). But steeper learning curve (3), API gated behind expensive plans, and less mature community features than Thinkific.

**Teachable:** Very easy to get started (5) with good TCO (4). But weaker on community (2), brand control (3), and scalability (3). Limited growth path compared to Thinkific.

**Podia:** Simplest and most affordable (5 on TCO). Great for getting started fast. But limited scalability (2), basic course delivery (3), and minimal brand control (3). Best for early-stage experts who want to launch quickly.

**Kartra:** Marketing powerhouse (5) with funnels, email, and automation. But course delivery is an afterthought (2), community is weak (2), and the platform has a steep learning curve (3). Best for experts who are marketers first, educators second.

## Scoring: Training Academies Persona

**Criteria** | **Thinkific** | **LearnWorlds** | **Docebo** | **Absorb LMS** | **TalentLMS** | **iSpring Learn**
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
1. Built-in E-commerce & Revenue Management | 5 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 2
2. Scalability & Performance Under Growth | 4 | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 3
3. Course Delivery & Blended Learning Support | 3 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 5
4. Branding, White-Label & Professional Appearance | 4 | 5 | 4 | 3 | 3 | 2
5. Certification & Compliance Management | 3 | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 5
6. Multi-Tenancy & Client Organization Management | 3 | 3 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 3
7. Reporting & Analytics | 3 | 3 | 5 | 5 | 3 | 4
8. Operational Automation & Admin Efficiency | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 3
9. Total Cost of Ownership | 4 | 3 | 2 | 2 | 5 | 4
10. Integrations & Tech Ecosystem | 4 | 3 | 5 | 5 | 3 | 3

### Scoring Rationale — Academies

**Thinkific:** Strongest e-commerce (5) — built natively for selling learning. Good scalability and integrations (4). Weaker on blended/ILT delivery (3), certification management (3), and multi-tenancy (3) compared to enterprise players. Best fit for digital-first academies.

**LearnWorlds:** Best course delivery (5) with interactive video and SCORM. Strongest white-label/branding (5). Good e-commerce (4). But expensive at scale, API gated behind higher plans, and multi-tenancy is limited (3).

**Docebo:** Enterprise powerhouse. Best at scalability (5), certification/compliance (5), multi-tenancy (5), reporting (5), and integrations (5). But e-commerce is adequate not exceptional (3), and TCO is high (2) with add-on pricing model.

**Absorb LMS:** Similar enterprise strength to Docebo. Strong on scalability, compliance, reporting, and integrations (all 5). Built-in eCommerce with 70+ payment gateways but less commerce-native than Thinkific. High TCO (2).

**TalentLMS:** Best TCO (5) — affordable and predictable. Good automation (4) and compliance (4). But limited e-commerce (3), basic reporting (3), and weaker integrations (3). Best for cost-conscious academies.

**iSpring Learn:** Excellent content authoring and blended learning (5) with strong compliance (5). But limited e-commerce (2), basic branding (2), and weaker scalability (3). Best for content-heavy training organizations.

## Scoring: Companies Adding Customer Training Persona

**Criteria** | **Thinkific Plus** | **Docebo** | **Absorb LMS** | **Skilljar** | **WorkRamp** | **Northpass**
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
1. Integration with Existing Business Systems | 4 | 5 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4
2. White-Label Branding & Seamless CX | 5 | 4 | 3 | 4 | 3 | 4
3. Ease of Setup & Admin (for Non-LMS Teams) | 5 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4
4. Scalability for External Audiences | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 3
5. Analytics Tied to Business Outcomes | 3 | 5 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 4
6. Learner Experience & Customer-Facing UX | 5 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4
7. Total Cost of Ownership | 4 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 3 | 4
8. Content Flexibility & Authoring | 4 | 4 | 4 | 3 | 4 | 3
9. Security, Compliance & Data Privacy | 4 | 5 | 5 | 4 | 4 | 3
10. Vendor Partnership & Implementation Speed | 4 | 3 | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4

### Scoring Rationale — Companies

**Thinkific Plus:** Best ease of setup (5), learner experience (5), and branding (5). The Thinker AI teaching assistant adds unique learner-facing value. Weaker on enterprise analytics (3) and deep integration breadth compared to Docebo/Absorb. SOC 2 Type 2 certified. Best fit for mid-market companies prioritizing customer experience and speed.

**Docebo:** Enterprise integration champion. Best at integrations (5), scalability (5), analytics (5), and security (5). FedRAMP Moderate certified. But harder to set up for non-LMS teams (3), less polished learner UX (3), and expensive (2). Best for larger enterprises with complex multi-audience needs.

**Absorb LMS:** Similar enterprise strength to Docebo. Strong integrations (5), scalability (5), and security (5). Headless LMS option (Infuse) for deep embedding. But same usability concerns for non-technical teams (3) and high TCO (2).

**Skilljar:** Purpose-built for customer education. Best analytics for customer outcomes (5) and strong integrations (5). But limited content authoring (3), moderate branding (4), and higher per-learner costs at scale (3).

**WorkRamp:** Solid all-rounder spanning customer + employee learning. Good across most criteria (4s). Not the absolute best at anything, but no major weaknesses. Growing platform.

**Northpass:** Good for mid-market with reasonable TCO (4). Strong on ease of admin (4) and vendor partnership (4). But limited scalability (3) and lighter security posture (3). Best for smaller customer training programs.

## Summary: Where Thinkific Wins and Loses by Persona

### Experts
**Thinkific wins on:** Commerce (5), community (5), scalability (5)
**Thinkific loses to:** Kajabi on ease of use and marketing; LearnWorlds on course delivery and branding

### Academies
**Thinkific wins on:** E-commerce (5), TCO (4)
**Thinkific loses to:** Docebo/Absorb on enterprise features; LearnWorlds on course delivery and branding; iSpring on blended learning

### Companies
**Thinkific wins on:** Branding (5), ease of setup (5), learner UX (5)
**Thinkific loses to:** Docebo/Absorb on integrations, analytics, scalability, and security depth; Skilljar on customer outcome analytics
