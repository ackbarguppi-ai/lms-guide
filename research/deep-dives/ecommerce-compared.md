# LMS E-Commerce & Sales Features Compared

*How eight major platforms handle payments, checkout, upsells, coupons, taxes, and affiliate programs — and which ones actually help you sell more.*

---

If you're building a learning business, the course content is only half the equation. The other half is getting paid — smoothly, reliably, and in ways that maximize revenue per customer. Not every LMS treats e-commerce equally. Some platforms were built as sales engines from day one. Others bolted on payment processing as an afterthought.

This guide breaks down the e-commerce and sales capabilities of eight leading platforms: **Thinkific, Kajabi, LearnWorlds, Teachable, Podia, Docebo, Absorb LMS, and TalentLMS**. We'll cover payment processing, checkout experience, upsells and order bumps, coupons and promotions, tax handling, and affiliate programs — so you can choose the platform that matches how you actually want to sell.

---

## The Quick Verdict

**Best overall e-commerce for experts selling courses:** Thinkific (TCommerce) and Teachable offer the most complete built-in sales stacks. Kajabi excels if you want an all-in-one marketing + sales system. LearnWorlds is strong on checkout customization.

**Best for enterprise/B2B course sales:** Docebo and Absorb LMS have purpose-built e-commerce modules for selling training at scale, with catalog management and bulk licensing.

**Most limited e-commerce:** TalentLMS has basic payment processing but lacks the depth of dedicated course-selling platforms.

## Payment Processing

Payment processing is foundational — it determines which payment methods your customers can use, how fast you get paid, and what fees you'll absorb.

### Thinkific

Thinkific's **TCommerce** is its proprietary payment system built on Stripe Connect. It processes payments in 130+ currencies and supports credit/debit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Buy Now Pay Later options (via Klarna and Afterpay in supported regions). TCommerce users receive daily payouts with no platform transaction fees — Thinkific only charges standard Stripe processing rates (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). For those who prefer direct Stripe or PayPal integration, Thinkific supports those as well, though TCommerce unlocks the full feature set including order bumps and one-click upsells.

### Kajabi

Kajabi uses **Kajabi Payments** (also Stripe-based) as its default processor, with support for PayPal as an alternative. Kajabi charges no transaction fees on any plan — you only pay standard Stripe processing rates. The platform supports credit cards, debit cards, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. Multi-currency support is available through Stripe's currency handling. Kajabi recently revamped its checkout with an enhanced system supporting multiple pricing options on a single offer page, making it easier to present payment plans alongside one-time pricing.

### Teachable

Teachable offers two payment gateway options: **Teachable:pay** (its native gateway) and custom payment gateways (direct Stripe or PayPal). Teachable:pay handles tax compliance, fraud protection, and supports credit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and PayPal. However, Teachable's transaction fee structure is plan-dependent and can be significant:

- **Free plan:** 10% + $1 per transaction
- **Basic plan:** 5% transaction fee
- **Pro plan:** No transaction fees
- **Pro+ plan:** No transaction fees

This tiered fee structure means Teachable can become expensive at lower plan levels, but competitive at Pro and above.

### LearnWorlds

LearnWorlds integrates with **Stripe, PayPal, Shopify Payments, and Apple Pay/Google Pay**. It offers a built-in checkout with no platform transaction fees on paid plans. LearnWorlds also supports Stripe Connect for marketplace-style setups. The platform recently added one-page checkout with instant payouts, making the buying experience faster for learners. Payment links allow you to share direct purchase URLs outside your school.

### Podia

Podia uses **Stripe** for payment processing and charges no transaction fees on its paid plans (the free plan charges 8%). It supports credit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and recently added global tax support. Podia's checkout is streamlined as a single-page experience, designed for simplicity over customization. Offline payments are supported for manual or invoice-based sales.

### Docebo

Docebo's e-commerce module integrates with **Stripe, PayPal, and Adyen** for payment processing. It also supports connections to **Shopify** for organizations that want to sell courses through an existing e-commerce storefront. Docebo is designed for B2B and enterprise sales, so it also supports invoice-based payments, purchase orders, and bulk licensing — features that expert-led platforms typically lack.

### Absorb LMS

Absorb LMS includes a built-in e-commerce module that integrates with **Stripe and PayPal**. It supports credit cards and multiple currencies. Absorb's e-commerce is positioned for organizations selling training externally — think certification programs, compliance training, and professional development courses. The platform supports catalog-based browsing with shopping cart functionality.

### TalentLMS

TalentLMS offers basic e-commerce with **Stripe and PayPal** integration. Payment processing is functional but minimal compared to dedicated course-selling platforms. TalentLMS supports setting prices for courses and bundles, processing payments, and issuing invoices. However, it lacks the checkout optimization features (upsells, order bumps, conversion tools) found on platforms like Thinkific and Kajabi.

## Checkout Experience

The checkout page is where revenue lives or dies. Conversion rate differences of even a few percentage points translate to thousands of dollars over time.

### Thinkific

TCommerce provides a **high-converting, customizable checkout** with features specifically designed to increase conversion:
- One-step checkout (no account creation required before purchase)
- Pre-populated fields for returning customers
- Multiple payment method display
- Mobile-optimized responsive design
- Checkout page customization through the site builder

Thinkific reports that TCommerce users sell up to 31% more than those using basic Stripe integration — largely due to the optimized checkout flow and additional sales tools it unlocks.

### Kajabi

Kajabi's **Enhanced Checkout** is a significant upgrade over its legacy system. It supports:
- Multiple pricing options on a single checkout page (one-time, payment plan, subscription)
- Customizable checkout templates
- Testimonials and trust badges on checkout pages
- Countdown timers for urgency
- Fully branded experience matching your site design

The checkout integrates tightly with Kajabi's pipeline and funnel system, making it easy to embed checkout into multi-step sales sequences.

### Teachable

Teachable's checkout is clean and straightforward with:
- Custom sales pages with embedded checkout
- Multi-step or single-step checkout options
- Coupon code fields
- Order summary display
- Mobile-responsive design

The checkout experience is solid but offers less customization than Kajabi or LearnWorlds.

### LearnWorlds

LearnWorlds recently overhauled its checkout with:
- **Single-page checkout** for faster conversions
- Payment link support for sharing purchase URLs
- Customizable checkout fields
- Cart functionality with multiple course purchases
- Stripe's optimized payment element for card entry

LearnWorlds supports a full shopping cart experience — not just individual course purchases — which is valuable if you sell multiple products.

### Podia

Podia's checkout emphasizes simplicity:
- Single-page, distraction-free checkout
- Minimal required fields
- Built-in upsell display at checkout
- Clean, branded design

It's effective for solo experts who want something that works without extensive configuration.

### Docebo, Absorb LMS, TalentLMS

Enterprise platforms handle checkout differently. Docebo and Absorb use **catalog-based storefronts** where learners browse courses, add to cart, and check out. The experience is functional but optimized for B2B purchasing (including multi-seat licenses and organizational billing) rather than high-conversion consumer sales. TalentLMS offers a basic storefront with course listings and checkout, adequate for internal or partner training sales.

## Upsells, Order Bumps & Revenue Maximizers

This is where platforms diverge significantly. Some have built entire revenue optimization systems; others stop at basic pricing.

### Thinkific

TCommerce includes:
- **Order bumps** — add-on products shown during checkout that customers can add with one click
- **One-click post-purchase upsells** — after the initial purchase, offer a complementary product with no need to re-enter payment details
- **Free trial to paid conversions** — start learners on a free trial and convert to paid
- **Flexible pricing:** one-time, subscriptions, payment plans, free, and custom pricing

Thinkific's order bump feature is particularly well-executed — it appears as a simple checkbox on the checkout page and has been shown to meaningfully increase average order value.

### Kajabi

Kajabi's upsell capabilities include:
- **Post-purchase upsells** through its offer system
- **One-click upsells** within sales pipelines
- **Offer bumps** at checkout
- **Pipeline (funnel) system** with built-in upsell/downsell sequences
- **Pricing options:** one-time, recurring, payment plans, free, pay-what-you-want

Kajabi's pipeline system is its secret weapon — you can build multi-step sales funnels with upsell and downsell pages built right into the flow, without third-party funnel software.

### Teachable

Teachable supports:
- **One-click upsells** (Pro plan and above)
- **Order bumps** at checkout
- **Pricing options:** one-time, subscriptions, payment plans
- Course bundles for packaging multiple products

### LearnWorlds

LearnWorlds offers:
- **Bundles and subscription packages**
- **Cross-selling** through related course suggestions
- **Pricing options:** one-time, subscriptions, payment plans, free
- No native one-click upsell feature comparable to Thinkific or Kajabi

### Podia

Podia recently enhanced its upsell capabilities:
- **Customizable upsells at checkout** — suggest related products during purchase
- **Bundles** for packaging products together
- **Pricing options:** one-time, payment plans, subscriptions, pay-what-you-want

### Enterprise Platforms

Docebo, Absorb, and TalentLMS focus on **bulk pricing, volume discounts, and subscription-based access** rather than consumer-style upsells. Docebo supports course catalogs with bundled pricing. Absorb offers preferred pricing for specific user groups. These aren't upsells in the marketing sense, but they serve revenue optimization for B2B sales.

## Tax Handling

Tax compliance is increasingly complex, especially for digital products sold globally. How a platform handles this can save you significant headaches.

### Thinkific

TCommerce includes **automated tax calculation and remittance** — one of the strongest selling points. Thinkific handles:
- Automatic sales tax, VAT, and GST calculation based on buyer location
- Tax collection at checkout
- Tax remittance to authorities on your behalf (for TCommerce users)
- Tax reporting and documentation

This is a genuine differentiator. Most platforms calculate taxes but leave remittance to you. TCommerce handles the full cycle.

### Kajabi

Kajabi calculates taxes based on buyer location through Stripe's tax tools, but **does not remit taxes on your behalf.** You're responsible for filing and paying collected taxes. Kajabi provides transaction reports for tax documentation.

### Teachable

Teachable:pay includes **automated tax calculation and collection** with EU VAT, US sales tax, and other jurisdiction handling. Like Thinkific, Teachable handles tax remittance for transactions processed through Teachable:pay — a significant advantage. BackOffice tax reporting is included for compliance documentation.

### LearnWorlds

LearnWorlds calculates taxes at checkout and supports manual tax rule configuration. It integrates with tax calculation services but **does not handle tax remittance.** You're responsible for filing.

### Podia

Podia recently added **global tax support** with automatic tax calculation based on buyer location. Tax reporting is available for compliance, but remittance is the seller's responsibility.

### Enterprise Platforms

Docebo, Absorb, and TalentLMS support **manual tax configuration** where admins set tax rates by region. Some integrate with external tax services. Enterprise buyers typically have their own tax compliance infrastructure, so automated remittance is less critical.

## Which Platform Fits Your Sales Model?

### For Solo Experts Selling Courses

**Thinkific** and **Teachable** deliver the strongest combination of checkout optimization, tax handling, and affiliate programs. Thinkific's TCommerce is particularly compelling with automated tax remittance, order bumps, and zero transaction fees. Teachable matches on tax remittance but charges transaction fees on lower plans.

**Kajabi** is the choice if you want built-in sales funnels and email marketing alongside your checkout — it's the closest to an all-in-one sales machine.

### For Academies Scaling Course Catalogs

**LearnWorlds** and **Thinkific** handle multi-course sales well with shopping cart functionality and catalog-style browsing. LearnWorlds' full cart checkout is valuable when learners might purchase multiple courses in one session.

### For Companies Selling Training Externally

**Docebo** and **Absorb LMS** were built for this. Bulk licensing, organizational billing, catalog management, and integration with CRM/ERP systems make them suited for B2B training sales at scale. They lack the consumer-facing upsell tools but excel at enterprise purchasing workflows.

### For Simplicity-First Sellers

**Podia** delivers a clean, no-fuss e-commerce experience. If you want to list products, process payments, and not think about checkout optimization, Podia gets out of your way. It won't maximize revenue per customer like Thinkific or Kajabi, but it also won't overwhelm you with options.

---

## The Bottom Line

E-commerce capabilities vary dramatically across LMS platforms, and the right choice depends on how you sell — not just what you sell. Consumer-facing experts need checkout optimization, upsells, and tax automation. Enterprise training organizations need bulk licensing and organizational billing. And some sellers just need a clean checkout that works.

The platforms that treat e-commerce as a core competency (Thinkific, Kajabi, Teachable) consistently outperform those that treat it as an add-on feature. If revenue growth is a priority — and it usually is — weight e-commerce capabilities heavily in your platform decision.
