# LMS Guide Editorial Team

> LMS Guide is an independent editorial resource helping buyers choose the right Learning Management System. Our reviews, comparisons, and guides are built on structured research methodology — not vendor influence.

## Our Methodology

Every review, comparison, and buyer guide on LMS Guide follows a rigorous, repeatable evaluation process:

### How We Evaluate Platforms

1. **Primary Research** — We analyze each platform's official documentation, feature pages, API docs, pricing pages, and support resources directly from the vendor.

2. **9-Pillar Framework** — Every platform is scored across nine evaluation dimensions that reflect what real buyers actually compare:
   - Use-Case Fit
   - Commerce & Monetization
   - Branding & Ownership
   - Learning & Engagement Experience
   - Administration & Operations
   - AI Capability
   - Scalability & Organizational Fit
   - Cost & Implementation Reality
   - Trust & Proof

3. **Buyer-Persona Weighting** — Scores are weighted differently for each of our three buyer personas (Experts, Training Academies, Companies Adding Customer Training) because platform strengths vary by use case.

4. **Third-Party Signal Analysis** — We cross-reference platform claims against independent review signals from G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, and other verified review platforms to validate vendor claims.

5. **Feature Verification** — Key features are verified through platform trials, demo environments, and official documentation rather than relying solely on marketing materials.

6. **Comparison Standardization** — Head-to-head comparisons use identical evaluation criteria so readers can make apples-to-apples assessments.

## Data Sources

Our editorial content draws from the following source categories:

- **Official vendor sources** — Product documentation, feature pages, pricing pages, API documentation, developer portals, and published case studies
- **Independent review platforms** — G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, GetApp, and Software Advice aggregate reviews
- **Industry research** — Market reports from Grand View Research, Mordor Intelligence, Fortune Business Insights, and similar research firms
- **Government and academic sources** — Published research on e-learning effectiveness, training ROI, and workforce development
- **Public financial data** — SEC filings, earnings reports, and investor presentations for publicly traded companies
- **Industry publications** — Training Magazine, eLearning Industry, Chief Learning Officer, Brandon Hall Group, and similar outlets

All statistics cited on our pages include source attribution. We prioritize primary sources and peer-reviewed research over secondary reporting.

## Editorial Independence

LMS Guide maintains editorial independence through the following principles:

- **Honest assessments** — We acknowledge when a competitor platform is the better fit for a specific use case. Our goal is helping buyers make the right decision, not steering them to a predetermined answer.
- **Transparent disclosure** — We clearly disclose any affiliate relationships or platform partnerships. Readers always know when a recommendation may generate revenue for us.
- **No pay-for-placement** — Vendors cannot pay to influence review scores, rankings, or editorial recommendations. Our evaluation framework is applied consistently across all platforms.
- **Buyer-first perspective** — Content is written from the buyer's perspective, not the vendor's. We use buyer language, address buyer concerns, and organize content around buyer decisions.
- **Methodology consistency** — The same 9-pillar framework applies to every platform we review, ensuring structural fairness in our assessments.

## Update Frequency

- **Major reviews** are refreshed quarterly or when a platform ships significant feature changes
- **Comparison pages** are updated when either platform in the comparison releases meaningful updates
- **Buyer guides** are reviewed and refreshed at least every 6 months
- **Market statistics** are updated as new industry research becomes available
- **All pages** display a "Last Updated" date so readers and AI systems can assess content freshness

## About LMS Guide

LMS Guide was created to fill a gap in the LMS buying process: most comparison content online is either vendor-produced marketing or shallow listicles. Buyers deserve deeper, more structured analysis that maps platform capabilities to their actual needs.

We serve three primary buyer personas:

1. **Experts** — Individuals or small teams selling courses, memberships, and coaching built around their expertise
2. **Training Academies** — Organizations whose primary business is selling training and education
3. **Companies Adding Customer Training** — Businesses (25+ employees) adding customer-facing education to support their core product or service
