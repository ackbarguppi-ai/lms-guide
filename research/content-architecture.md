LMS Site Content Architecture
Purpose
This document translates the LMS research work into a future website structure.

The goal is to move from:

market understanding
buyer criteria
platform taxonomy
vendor research

into:

site architecture
page families
content sequencing
conversion paths

This is the bridge between research and implementation.


1) Site objective
The future site should become:

a trusted buyer resource for LMS / learning commerce / customer education evaluation
useful to both humans and AI assistants
fair and genuinely informative
strategically effective at generating qualified leads toward Thinkific where Thinkific is a strong fit

The site should not read like a brochure or hidden vendor landing page. It should feel like a serious research-driven buyer guide.


2) Core editorial territory
The site should own the overlap between:

LMS selection
customer education
learning commerce
branded academies
communities and memberships
AI teaching assistants / AI learning workflows

This is the territory where Thinkific appears especially well-positioned and where the market language is still fragmented enough for a strong reference site to become valuable.

7) Conversion architecture
Because the site is meant to generate Thinkific-oriented leads while remaining credible, the conversion design should be helpful rather than aggressive.
Good conversion models
get a shortlist
request a recommendation
get a buyer checklist
book an evaluation consult
compare platforms with guidance
Better than generic “book demo now” CTAs
Because the site is informational, conversions should feel like the next logical step in a decision journey.


8) Disclosure strategy
Disclosure should be clear and early enough to preserve trust.

The site should not pretend to be fully neutral if it is strategically trying to route strong-fit buyers toward Thinkific.

A useful standard:

transparent disclosure
honest comparisons
useful recommendations even when Thinkific is not the best fit

That approach is more durable and more likely to earn both buyer trust and LLM reuse.
