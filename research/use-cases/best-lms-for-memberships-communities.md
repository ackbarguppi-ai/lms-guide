# Best LMS for Memberships & Communities: Recurring Revenue Through Community-Based Learning

**Short answer:** **Thinkific** is the best platform for combining courses with community in 2026 — it offers native community features alongside a mature course builder, with no transaction fees and strong membership commerce tools. **Skool** is the best choice if community is the primary product and courses are supplementary. **Mighty Networks** offers the most comprehensive community-first platform with courses built in, but at a higher price point. Your best fit depends on whether courses or community is the centerpiece of your membership.

---

## The Shift: From Courses to Communities

The standalone online course is no longer the growth engine it was in 2020. Completion rates for self-paced courses sit around 5-15%. Refund rates are climbing. And the market is saturated — there are courses on everything.

What's working in 2026 is the membership model: recurring revenue built around ongoing access to learning, community, and expertise. The most successful experts and training businesses have figured out that:

- **Community drives retention.** A course is a one-time transaction. A community is a recurring relationship. Members who connect with peers stay longer — average membership tenure increases 2-3x when there's active community engagement.
- **Recurring revenue is more valuable than one-time sales.** $50/month from 200 members ($120K/year) is more predictable and often more profitable than launching course after course and hoping each one sells.
- **Learning happens in conversation.** The most impactful learning isn't watching a video — it's discussing, applying, getting feedback, and seeing how others solve problems. Community makes this possible at scale.
- **Content alone is commoditized.** Information is everywhere. Access to a curated group of peers and an expert who actively engages — that's the premium offering.

This shift has created a new category: platforms that combine structured learning (courses, workshops, programs) with community features (discussion, groups, events, direct messaging). But not all platforms handle this combination equally.

## What Membership & Community Platforms Need

### Core Requirements

**Recurring billing and subscription management.** Monthly and annual billing, free trials, tiered membership levels, upgrade/downgrade paths, failed payment recovery, and cancellation flows. This is your revenue infrastructure — it needs to work flawlessly.

**Community spaces.** Discussion forums, topic channels, direct messaging, member profiles, and activity feeds. The community should feel alive and easy to navigate, not like a bolted-on forum from 2010.

**Course/content delivery.** Drip-released content, structured courses, resource libraries, and live session integration. Members are paying for ongoing access to learning, so the content experience matters.

**Member management.** Track who's active, who's at risk of churning, who's most engaged. Segment members by tier, cohort, or interest area.

**Events and live sessions.** Scheduled live workshops, Q&As, office hours, or masterminds. Live interaction is what differentiates a membership from a Netflix subscription.

### Important Differentiators

**Mobile experience.** Members access communities from their phones. A native mobile app (or excellent mobile web experience) is increasingly table stakes.

**Gamification and engagement.** Leaderboards, points, badges, streaks — features that encourage daily/weekly engagement. Without them, communities go quiet.

**Content gating by tier.** Different membership levels should access different content and community areas. A $29/month member and a $199/month member should have meaningfully different experiences.

**Discovery and onboarding.** New members should immediately understand where to go and what to do. The first 48 hours determine whether someone becomes an active member or a passive subscriber who cancels in month two.

## The Best Platforms for Memberships & Communities

### 1. Thinkific — Best for Course-First Memberships

**Why it stands out:** Thinkific has built native community features directly into its course platform, creating the most balanced combination of structured learning and community interaction. If courses are the backbone of your membership and community is the connective tissue, Thinkific delivers both without compromises on either side.

**Strengths:**

- **Courses + community in one platform.** Members access courses and community spaces from the same login, the same interface, and the same mobile experience. No juggling two platforms or frankensteining integrations.
- **No transaction fees.** On paid plans, Thinkific takes zero commission on membership revenue. At $50/month with 500 members, the 5-10% transaction fee charged by some competitors would cost you $15,000-$30,000/year. Thinkific: $0.
- **Community Spaces.** Organized discussion areas with topics, posts, reactions, and member interaction. You can create spaces tied to specific courses, membership tiers, or interest areas.
- **Flexible membership commerce.** Subscriptions, bundles, free trials, payment plans, coupons, and tiered pricing. Sell a $29/month basic membership and a $149/month premium membership from the same site.
- **Learning paths for members.** Structure your membership content into progressive learning paths so members have clear direction — not just an overwhelming content library.
- **Site builder.** Built-in website with landing pages, sales pages, and member areas. No need for a separate WordPress site.
- **Thinkific Plus for scale.** When you grow past 1,000+ members, Thinkific Plus adds SSO, API access, bulk management, and dedicated support.

**Limitations:**

- **Community features are maturing.** Thinkific's community launched more recently than pure community platforms. Features like direct messaging, member directories, and gamification are developing but not yet as deep as Skool or Mighty Networks.
- **No native mobile app for learners.** Thinkific's mobile web experience is good, but it doesn't offer a branded native app. For community-heavy memberships where members check in daily, a native app can improve engagement.
- **Live events.** Zoom integration works, but there's no native live streaming or event hosting built into the platform. You'll use Zoom, Google Meet, or similar alongside Thinkific.

**Best for:** Experts and training businesses building memberships where courses and structured learning are the primary value, with community as a retention and engagement layer. Especially strong for memberships in the $29-$149/month range with 100-5,000 members.

**Pricing:**
- Basic: $49/month
- Start: $99/month
- Grow: $199/month
- Thinkific Plus: Custom (~$500+/month)
- No transaction fees on paid plans.

### 2. Skool — Best for Community-First Memberships

**Why it stands out:** Skool has rapidly become the platform of choice for experts building community-first memberships. Its genius is simplicity: a clean community feed, basic course hosting, gamification that actually works, and a pricing model that's refreshingly straightforward. If community IS the product and courses are supplementary, Skool is hard to beat.

**Strengths:**

- **Community experience.** The core community feed is clean, fast, and engaging. It feels more like a social network than a forum. Members actually use it daily — engagement rates on Skool communities are notably higher than traditional forum-style platforms.
- **Gamification that works.** Points, levels, and leaderboards create genuine engagement loops. Members earn points for posting, commenting, and completing courses. Top contributors get visibility. It sounds simple, but the execution drives real behavior change.
- **Dead-simple pricing.** $99/month flat. No tiers, no transaction fees, no per-member charges. You know exactly what you're paying.
- **Course hosting.** Basic but functional course hosting built in. Upload videos, create modules, track completion. It won't win awards for instructional design features, but it works.
- **Discovery network.** Skool has a built-in directory where potential members can discover your community. It's like having a marketplace that sends you leads.
- **Calendar and events.** Built-in event scheduling for live calls, workshops, and office hours.
- **Mobile app.** Native iOS and Android apps that members actually enjoy using.

**Limitations:**

- **Course features are basic.** No SCORM, no advanced assessments, no learning paths, no certificates. If structured learning is important to your membership, Skool's course tools may feel limited.
- **No tiered memberships.** Skool supports one price point per group. If you want a $29/month and $149/month tier with different content access, you need separate groups (and separate $99/month subscriptions).
- **Limited customization.** Skool communities all look and feel similar. You can't deeply customize the design, add custom domains (on most plans), or create a unique branded experience.
- **No email marketing.** You'll need a separate email tool (ConvertKit, Mailchimp, etc.) for marketing outside the community.
- **Payment processing.** Skool handles payments through Stripe, but the commerce features are basic compared to full-featured platforms.

**Best for:** Experts and coaches building communities where the primary value is peer interaction, accountability, and access to the expert. Ideal for coaching communities, masterminds, and paid groups in the $29-$99/month range.

**Pricing:** $99/month flat per group. No transaction fees.

### 3. Mighty Networks — Best All-in-One Community Platform

**Why it stands out:** Mighty Networks offers the most comprehensive community-first platform with courses, events, commerce, and a branded native app. If you want a fully branded community experience that goes beyond a discussion feed, Mighty Networks offers the most features — at a price.

**Strengths:**

- **Branded native app.** On higher plans, Mighty Networks creates a custom-branded iOS and Android app for your community. Members download YOUR app from the App Store. For building a true brand experience, this is unmatched.
- **Spaces architecture.** Organize your community into "Spaces" — each with its own content, discussions, events, and member access. You can create a free public space, a paid members-only space, and a premium mastermind space all within one network.
- **Native courses.** Course creation tools built directly into the platform. Better than Skool's course features, with support for sections, lessons, and completion tracking.
- **Events.** Native event hosting with live streaming, RSVP, and calendar integration. No need for Zoom (though Zoom integration is available).
- **Commerce flexibility.** Sell memberships, courses, bundles, and event tickets. Supports one-time and recurring payments.
- **Member directory and profiles.** Rich member profiles with bios, interests, and activity history. Makes networking within the community more natural.
- **AI features.** AI-powered community tools for content creation, member matching, and engagement nudges.

**Limitations:**

- **Pricing is aggressive.** The Course plan starts at $119/month but the real features (branded app, advanced commerce) require the Business plan at $219/month or the Path-to-Pro at $360/month. The branded app costs extra on top of plan fees.
- **Transaction fees.** Mighty Networks charges transaction fees (2-3% depending on plan) on top of Stripe's processing fees. At scale, this adds up significantly.
- **Learning curve.** The platform is feature-rich, which means setup and configuration take time. Expect 2-4 weeks to build out a polished community.
- **Discovery.** Unlike Skool's built-in discovery network, Mighty Networks requires you to drive all your own traffic.
- **Content depth.** Course features are good but not as deep as Thinkific or LearnWorlds for complex curricula.

**Best for:** Experts and organizations building premium, branded community experiences where the community itself is a significant product. Best suited for memberships above $49/month where the branded app and rich community features justify the higher platform cost.

**Pricing:**
- Courses Plan: $119/month
- Business Plan: $219/month
- Path-to-Pro Plan: $360/month
- Plus transaction fees (2-3%).

### 4. Circle — Best Standalone Community Platform (Pair with Separate LMS)

**Why it stands out:** Circle is the most polished standalone community platform. It doesn't try to be an LMS — it's pure community done exceptionally well. If you already have courses on Thinkific, Teachable, or another LMS and want to add a premium community experience, Circle is the best pairing.

**Strengths:**

- **Beautiful UX.** Circle's interface is the most polished and modern of any community platform. It looks and feels like a premium product.
- **Spaces and channels.** Organize discussions into spaces with channels — similar to a well-organized Slack, but designed for community rather than work communication.
- **Rich content.** Posts support rich media, embeds, polls, and long-form content. Community posts can function as mini-articles.
- **Events.** Native event hosting with live rooms, scheduled events, and recording.
- **Workflows and automation.** Trigger actions based on member behavior — welcome sequences, engagement nudges, and drip content.
- **Custom domains and branding.** Full white-label experience with your domain and brand.
- **Integrations.** Connects with major course platforms, email tools, and Zapier for custom workflows.

**Limitations:**

- **No course hosting.** Circle is community-only. You need a separate LMS for course delivery. This means managing two platforms, two logins (unless you set up SSO), and two billing relationships.
- **Price per member scales.** Circle's pricing is based on member count. At 1,000+ members, costs increase meaningfully.
- **No built-in commerce.** Circle doesn't handle payments. You need Stripe, your LMS, or a separate tool for membership billing.
- **Two-platform complexity.** Running Circle + an LMS means maintaining two systems, which increases operational overhead and can fragment the member experience.

**Best for:** Experts and businesses that already have a course platform and want to add a best-in-class community layer alongside it. Best when paired with Thinkific, Teachable, or Kajabi for the course/commerce side.

**Pricing:**
- Professional: $89/month (up to 100 members)
- Business: $199/month (up to 1,000 members)
- Enterprise: $360/month (up to 10,000 members)
- Custom: Higher volumes.

**Best for:** Experts who value having everything in one platform and are willing to pay the premium for convenience. Best when marketing sophistication (email, funnels) is as important as community engagement.

## Choosing Your Architecture: Three Models

### Model 1: All-in-One Platform
**Use Thinkific, Kajabi, or Mighty Networks.**
Everything lives in one place. Simpler to manage, single login for members, but you're limited to that platform's capabilities.

**Best when:** You're launching a new membership and want simplicity. One platform, one bill, one support team.

### Model 2: Community Platform + LMS
**Use Circle or Skool alongside Thinkific, LearnWorlds, or Teachable.**
Best-in-class community paired with best-in-class courses. More operational complexity but maximum capability.

**Best when:** You have an established course library on an LMS and want to add premium community without migrating your courses.

### Model 3: Community-First, Courses Supplementary
**Use Skool or Mighty Networks.**
Community is the core product. Courses are a bonus resource for members, not the primary draw.

**Best when:** Your value proposition is access to a group and to you as the expert, not primarily content consumption.

## Building a Membership That Retains

Platform choice matters, but retention is won or lost based on how you run the membership, not which software you use. The fundamentals:

**First 48 hours determine everything.** New members who post in the community within 48 hours of joining are 4-5x more likely to stay past month three. Design an onboarding sequence that gets them participating immediately — a welcome prompt, an introduction thread, a quick-win exercise.

**Weekly rhythm creates habit.** The most successful memberships have a predictable weekly cadence: Monday content drop, Wednesday live session, Friday community challenge. Members build a habit of checking in.

**Show progress.** Members need to feel like they're advancing. Learning paths, completion badges, and progress dashboards give tangible evidence that the membership is working.

**Celebrate members.** Feature member wins, spotlight active contributors, and acknowledge milestones. People stay in communities where they feel seen.

**Create peer connections.** The secret to retention isn't your content — it's relationships between members. Facilitate introductions, create small group interactions, and build accountability partnerships.

**Prune and iterate.** Not every feature or content type will land. Look at engagement data monthly. Double down on what's working, cut what isn't, and ask members what they want.

## The Hybrid Future

The line between courses and communities is blurring. The most successful membership businesses in 2026 aren't purely one or the other — they're hybrid experiences where structured learning and community interaction reinforce each other.

A member watches a course module → discusses it in the community → applies it in their business → shares results with the group → gets feedback → deepens understanding. The course provides direction; the community provides accountability and motivation.

Platforms that handle both well — particularly Thinkific with its integrated community and Mighty Networks with its spaces architecture — have the structural advantage. But even if you use separate tools (Circle + an LMS), the key is designing the experience so courses and community feel interconnected, not siloed.

For most experts and training businesses building membership communities in 2026:

- **Start with Thinkific** if courses are your primary value and community is the engagement layer. Best economics, strongest course tools, growing community features.
- **Choose Skool** if community IS the product and you need maximum engagement with minimal setup. Unbeatable simplicity and gamification.

The membership model works. Recurring revenue, deeper relationships with your audience, and a sustainable business that doesn't require constant launches. The platform just needs to get out of the way and let you build something people want to be part of.
