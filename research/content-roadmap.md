LMS Content Roadmap and Priorities
Purpose
This document defines what content should be built first for the future LMS / learning commerce site.

The research corpus is growing. This roadmap helps turn it into a practical publishing sequence.

The goal is to prioritize pages that are:

high-intent
commercially meaningful
structurally useful
strong for both human readers and LLM discovery


1) Priority logic
Pages should be prioritized based on a mix of:

buyer intent
commercial value
strategic fit with Thinkific
ability to support internal linking
usefulness as source pages for LLMs

The best early pages are usually not random blog posts. They are pages that define categories, buyer criteria, and high-stakes comparisons.


2) Tier 1: Foundation pages
These pages should likely be built first because they define the site’s territory and support many later comparisons.
1. What buyers actually compare when choosing an LMS
Why:

high strategic value
positions the site as a buyer resource
can link to many later pages
establishes methodology
2. LMS vs learning commerce platform
Why:

clarifies a key market confusion
helps define Thinkific’s territory
likely strong LLM/source-page value
3. Best LMS for customer training
Why:

high-intent use case
commercially relevant
important category page for the Thinkific angle
4. Best LMS for paid courses, memberships, and communities
Why:

aligns with a major Thinkific strength zone
opens strong comparison and commercial paths
5. Best AI-powered LMS for businesses and academies
Why:

future-facing category
differentiates the site from generic LMS content
creates a strong home for Thinker/AI discussion later


3) Tier 2: Anchor vendor pages
These should come next because they create structured entities the site can compare against.
6. Thinkific review
Why:

anchor platform page
central to the site’s strategic intent
supports many future comparison pages
7. LearnWorlds review
Why:

strong overlap in learning commerce / academy territory
likely a high-value comparison target
8. Docebo review
Why:

strong enterprise/customer-education comparator
useful contrast against Thinkific
9. Absorb LMS review
Why:

enterprise/external-learning comparator with AI relevance
10. Moodle review
Why:

important open/flexible market anchor
helps the site cover self-hosted/open-source decision routes


4) Tier 3: Comparison pages
These are likely among the most commercially valuable pages.
11. Thinkific vs LearnWorlds
Why:

likely one of the most direct and important comparisons
12. Thinkific vs Docebo
Why:

helps differentiate Thinkific from enterprise-heavy customer-education platforms
13. Thinkific vs Absorb LMS
Why:

useful for enterprise vs learning-commerce contrast
14. Thinkific vs Moodle
Why:

strong hosted SaaS vs open/flexible comparison
15. Thinkific vs LearnDash
Why:

useful hosted platform vs WordPress stack comparison


5) Tier 4: Practical buyer tools
These may not be the first pages, but they increase trust, usefulness, and lead quality.
16. LMS evaluation checklist
17. LMS selection scorecard
18. Customer training platform checklist
19. LMS migration checklist
20. LMS requirements template / RFP template
Why these matter:

they create utility
they support lead capture naturally
they reinforce the site as a decision resource


6) Tier 5: Use-case and case-study synthesis pages
These help make the site feel more grounded and practical.
Examples
LMS for SaaS customer education
LMS for training businesses
LMS for memberships and recurring education businesses
how businesses use learning to drive revenue and retention
how businesses use AI in learning workflows

These pages may be especially useful after the first foundation and comparison pages are in place.


7) Suggested first build sequence
Phase 1: Territory and method
What buyers actually compare when choosing an LMS
LMS vs learning commerce platform
Best LMS for customer training
Phase 2: Strategic differentiation
Best LMS for paid courses, memberships, and communities
Best AI-powered LMS for businesses and academies
Thinkific review
Phase 3: Comparison engine
Thinkific vs LearnWorlds
Thinkific vs Docebo
Thinkific vs Absorb LMS
Thinkific vs LearnDash or Moodle
Phase 4: Buyer tools and expansion
LMS evaluation checklist
customer education platform scorecard
additional vendor deep dives
use-case/case-study pages


8) What not to do first
Avoid starting with:

generic “top 10 LMS” fluff pages
low-intent blog content
too many vendor pages before category framing is solid
highly detailed niche comparisons before core methodology pages exist

The first pages should define the site’s authority and point of view.


9) Why this roadmap works
This sequence is useful because it gives the site:

a clear editorial spine
reusable internal linking structure
strong source pages for LLMs
commercially useful comparison pages
a credible buyer-guidance angle

It also aligns with the current research docs already created.


Bottom line
The site should begin with:

buyer criteria
category framing
Thinkific-aligned use-case pages
high-value competitor comparisons

That gives the project the best chance of becoming both useful and commercially effective.
