# Kajabi vs LearnWorlds (2026): Which Platform Actually Fits Your Business?

Kajabi is an all-in-one business platform with built-in marketing, email, and sales funnels — best for experts who want everything under one roof. LearnWorlds is a learning-first platform with interactive video, SCORM support, and advanced assessments — best for training businesses that prioritize the student experience. Kajabi starts at $89/month; LearnWorlds starts at $29/month.

That's the short version. The rest of this guide breaks down exactly where each platform wins, where it falls short, and which one makes sense for your specific situation.

## Category-by-Category Breakdown

### Course Creation & Learning Experience

**LearnWorlds wins this category.**

LearnWorlds was built as a learning management system first. Its course builder supports interactive video with in-player quizzes, clickable hotspots, and chapter markers. You can upload SCORM/xAPI packages, create multimedia e-books, assign graded assessments, and issue certificates through Credly or Accredible. Gamification features (points, badges, leaderboards) are built in. The AI assistant generates course outlines, titles, and descriptions.

Course structure is modular: sections contain learning activities including video, audio, PDFs, live sessions (Zoom, Teams, Webex), SCORM modules, and assignment uploads.

Kajabi's course builder is competent but more straightforward. You organize content into modules and lessons, upload video/audio/text/PDFs, add quizzes, and drip content on a schedule. Templates like Momentum, Creative, and Prosper give courses a polished look with minimal effort. Cohort-based courses work well for challenges and guided programs.

Where Kajabi falls short: no SCORM support, no interactive video overlays, limited assessment depth, and no third-party certificate integrations. If your students need to demonstrate compliance or earn accredited credentials, Kajabi doesn't have the tooling.

**Bottom line:** LearnWorlds for deep educational experiences. Kajabi for fast, polished course launches.

### Marketing, Email & Sales Funnels

**Kajabi wins this category — decisively.**

Marketing is Kajabi's strongest suit. The platform includes:

- **Email marketing** with campaigns, broadcasts, drip sequences, A/B subject testing, and embedded video/countdown timers
- **Visual funnel builder** for automated workflows from opt-in to sale
- **Unlimited landing pages** and opt-in forms
- **CRM** with contact tagging, custom fields, and behavioral automations
- **Abandoned cart emails** and post-purchase sequences
- **Kajabi Payments** supporting credit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Afterpay, and Klarna
- **AI-powered tools** for generating marketing copy, social posts, and email templates

Kajabi takes zero commission on sales — only standard payment processing fees apply.

LearnWorlds handles checkout natively (one-time, subscription, and installment payments across Stripe, PayPal, and Shopify integrations) and includes a built-in affiliate engine. But there's no built-in email marketing, no visual funnel builder, and no CRM. You'll need ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, or a similar tool for campaigns. LearnWorlds connects to these via native integrations and Zapier.

**Bottom line:** If marketing automation matters to your business, Kajabi eliminates the need for a separate email platform, funnel builder, and CRM. LearnWorlds requires you to build that stack yourself.

### Website Building & Branding

**Tie — depends on your priorities.**

Kajabi provides professionally designed templates, a full site builder with blog and podcast pages, a theme editor, and global design settings. It's polished and easy to use with minimal design skill.

LearnWorlds offers a drag-and-drop editor with a widget system, customizable themes, and a dynamic page builder. Higher plans include white-label options and deeper CSS customization. The site builder is more flexible but has a steeper learning curve.

Both platforms support custom domains and SSL certificates at no extra cost.

**Bottom line:** Kajabi for quick, professional sites. LearnWorlds for agencies or training businesses that need granular brand control and white-labeling.

### Community & Engagement

**Kajabi wins.**

Kajabi's built-in community features function like a private social network: posts, topics, challenges, member groups, and gamification elements. Communities integrate directly with courses and email automations. On the Growth plan and above, you get robust group management tools.

LearnWorlds offers discussion boards inside courses and a community add-on, but it's more basic. Social features exist but aren't the platform's core strength.

**Bottom line:** If community is central to your offer (memberships, group coaching, challenges), Kajabi's integrated approach is stronger.

### Analytics & Reporting

**LearnWorlds wins for learning analytics. Kajabi wins for business analytics.**

LearnWorlds provides detailed content-level analytics: learner engagement, completion rates, time-on-task, assessment scores, and behavioral metrics (clicks, scroll tracking). This is useful for training businesses that need to demonstrate learning outcomes or optimize curriculum.

Kajabi's analytics focus on business performance: daily/weekly/monthly revenue, funnel conversion rates, email open/click rates, average order value, and individual contact journey tracking. Its dashboards connect directly with the marketing and funnel tools.

**Bottom line:** Two different strengths. Pick based on whether you need to optimize learning outcomes or sales performance.

### Integrations & Extensibility

**LearnWorlds has a slight edge.**

LearnWorlds integrates with Zapier, Make, Pabbly, and Integrately for custom workflows. It supports SCORM/xAPI imports, offers an open API, and connects natively with major email platforms, CRMs, and analytics tools. Higher plans include webhook support.

Kajabi has 185+ integrations (vs LearnWorlds' ~49), but many are through Zapier rather than native connections. Kajabi's API is available only on the Pro plan ($399/month), which limits extensibility for mid-tier users.

**Bottom line:** LearnWorlds is more open by default. Kajabi locks advanced integration behind its highest plan.

### Support & Onboarding

**Kajabi wins.**

Kajabi offers 24/7 live chat support on Growth and Pro plans, plus Kajabi University (self-paced training), webinars, documentation, and a community forum. The onboarding experience is well-structured.

LearnWorlds provides email support (24/5 on Starter, 24/7 on higher plans), a knowledge base, video tutorials, and onboarding assistance on premium plans. Priority support with a dedicated account manager is available on Learning Center and above.

**Bottom line:** Kajabi's support is faster and more accessible, especially on mid-tier plans.

## Who Should Choose Kajabi

Kajabi is the stronger choice if you match one or more of these profiles:

### Experts Selling Courses and Coaching
You're an expert with a personal brand, selling online courses, group coaching, or digital products. You want to handle marketing, email, checkout, and community without juggling multiple tools. You'd rather pay more for convenience than spend time connecting systems. Kajabi's Growth plan ($199/month) is the sweet spot — it unlocks affiliates, advanced automations, and enough products/funnels for a multi-offer business.

### Experts Scaling to Multiple Offers
You have (or plan to have) several courses, a membership, coaching packages, and digital downloads. Kajabi's unified product catalog and sales pipeline builder make it straightforward to cross-sell and upsell across your entire catalog.

### Businesses That Prioritize Marketing Over Pedagogy
If your primary challenge is getting people to buy (not optimizing the learning experience), Kajabi's built-in marketing suite will serve you better than LearnWorlds plus a stack of external tools.

## Who Should Choose LearnWorlds

LearnWorlds is the stronger choice if you match one or more of these profiles:

### Training Academies and Professional Educators
You run a training business, bootcamp, or academy where learning outcomes matter as much as revenue. You need interactive video, graded assessments, certificates, SCORM compliance, and detailed learner analytics. LearnWorlds' Pro Trainer plan ($99/month) delivers serious LMS capabilities at a fraction of enterprise pricing.

### Companies Adding Customer or Employee Training
You're adding a training program to an existing business — onboarding courses, product certification, or compliance training. You need white-labeling, multiple admin users, and the ability to track learner progress at a granular level. LearnWorlds' Learning Center plan ($299/month) handles this with 25 admin seats and full brand control.

### Budget-Conscious Experts Testing the Market
You're launching your first course and want to validate demand before committing to a $200/month platform. LearnWorlds' Starter plan ($29/month with the $5/sale fee) lets you get live quickly. The 30-day free trial (no credit card required) is twice as long as Kajabi's 14-day trial.

### Organizations Needing SCORM or Compliance Features
If your training content is packaged in SCORM or xAPI format — common in corporate training, healthcare, and regulated industries — Kajabi simply doesn't support it. LearnWorlds does, starting at the Pro Trainer level.

## The Verdict

These platforms solve different problems. The "which is better" question only makes sense once you define what "better" means for your business.

**Choose Kajabi if** you're an expert or small team building a brand around courses, coaching, and digital products — and you want marketing, email, funnels, community, and payments handled in one platform without stitching tools together. You'll pay more per month, but you'll spend less time on tech plumbing.

**Choose LearnWorlds if** you're running a training business, academy, or corporate learning program where the quality of the learning experience is a competitive advantage. Interactive video, SCORM support, granular analytics, and advanced assessments are things Kajabi simply doesn't offer. You'll need to bring your own marketing stack, but the learning tools are substantially deeper.

**For most experts selling online courses and coaching**, Kajabi is the more practical choice — not because it's better technology, but because its all-in-one approach eliminates the integration tax that eats up time and money.

**For training academies, companies running certification programs, and organizations with compliance requirements**, LearnWorlds is the clear winner. Its learning-focused feature set is in a different league.

Neither platform is the wrong choice. The wrong choice is picking a marketing platform when you need an LMS, or picking an LMS when you need a marketing engine.
