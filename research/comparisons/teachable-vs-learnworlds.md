# Teachable vs LearnWorlds: Which Platform Fits Your Business?

Teachable and LearnWorlds both help you build and sell online courses — but they solve different problems. Teachable prioritizes simplicity and speed-to-launch. LearnWorlds prioritizes depth of learning tools and site customization.

**The short version:** Choose Teachable if you want the fastest path from idea to published course with minimal setup. Choose LearnWorlds if you need interactive video, SCORM compliance, built-in website building, or white-label mobile apps.

This comparison breaks down pricing, features, and tradeoffs across every category that matters — so you can pick the right platform for how you actually plan to use it.

**Key detail:** Teachable's Basic plan charges a 5% transaction fee on every sale. At $1,200/month in revenue, those fees ($60) equal the cost difference of upgrading to the Builder plan — making the upgrade a break-even decision at that point.

### LearnWorlds Plans (2026)

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Transaction Fee | Admin Users |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | $29 | $24 | $5 per sale | 1 |
| **Pro Trainer** | $99 | $79 | None | 5 |
| **Learning Center** | $299 | $249 | None | 25 |
| **High Volume/Corporate** | Custom | Custom | None | Custom |

**Key detail:** LearnWorlds' Starter plan charges a flat $5 per course sale (not a percentage). If your average course costs $50, that's a 10% effective fee. The Pro Trainer plan eliminates this entirely and becomes more cost-effective once you sell more than ~14 courses per month.

### Pricing Verdict

For budget-conscious beginners, LearnWorlds' Starter plan ($24/mo annual) is cheaper upfront than Teachable's Basic ($29/mo annual), but the $5-per-sale fee can add up fast. Teachable's percentage-based fee (5%) scales better for higher-priced courses. Both platforms remove transaction fees at roughly the $79–$99/month tier. At the mid-range, pricing is comparable; at the high end, LearnWorlds' Learning Center ($249/mo) undercuts Teachable's Advanced ($309/mo) while offering more features like white-label apps and SSO.

## Category-by-Category Breakdown

### Course Creation & Content Tools

**Teachable** keeps course building simple. You organize content into sections and lessons, drag-and-drop videos, PDFs, audio files, and text blocks, and publish. The builder is linear and intuitive — most people can have a basic course live within an hour. Teachable also includes AI-powered tools for generating course outlines, curricula, and marketing copy, which helps speed up the ideation phase.

However, the simplicity has limits. Quiz options are basic (multiple-choice and short-answer only). There's no support for SCORM packages, interactive video elements, or formal assessments. If you build courseware in tools like Articulate Storyline or Adobe Captivate, Teachable can't host those files natively.

**LearnWorlds** offers a significantly deeper course creation toolkit. Beyond standard video and text lessons, you get:

- **Interactive video:** Overlay quizzes, questions, buttons, and notes directly on video timelines. Students engage during playback rather than passively watching.
- **SCORM 1.2 and HTML5 support:** Upload packages built in external authoring tools with full progress tracking and graded completion.
- **E-book builder:** Create interactive e-books directly within the platform.
- **Formal assessments:** Timed exams, question pools, randomized questions, pass/fail thresholds.
- **Certificates:** Auto-generated certificates of completion with customizable templates.
- **Auto-transcripts:** AI-generated video transcripts available on higher plans.

The tradeoff is complexity. LearnWorlds' builder has more moving parts, and users report that the layout can feel rigid — template-based sections with extra whitespace that's hard to adjust. Setup takes longer, but the output is more polished for structured learning programs.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — for any use case beyond basic video courses. Teachable wins on speed-to-launch for simple content.

### Website Building & Customization

**Teachable** provides a basic block-based site editor. You can add your logo, brand colors, and custom domain, but layout control is limited. There are no page templates, no native blogging tools, and design customization requires CSS knowledge or a developer. Most Teachable users end up using an external landing page builder (like Leadpages or Carrd) for their marketing pages.

**LearnWorlds** includes a full visual page builder with drag-and-drop sections, pre-designed templates, popups, header/footer controls, and a built-in blog with SEO features. You can build landing pages, sales pages, and content pages that look professional without leaving the platform. The builder supports animations, custom fonts, and detailed section-level styling.

LearnWorlds also offers stronger white-labeling: custom domains, removal of LearnWorlds branding, and on the Learning Center plan, full school cloning and branded mobile apps. However, some users note that the builder's design tools can feel finicky — moving images and formatting text is occasionally clunkier than expected.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — by a significant margin. If you want your course site to look like a real website rather than a template, LearnWorlds is the clear choice.

### Student Engagement & Community

**Teachable** launched its built-in community feature, allowing discussion spaces tied to courses. Students can interact, ask questions, and build connections within the Teachable ecosystem. The community feature is available across all plans. Teachable also offers a dedicated coaching product type — you can create intake forms, set milestones, and manage one-on-one coaching clients directly on the platform. This is a genuine differentiator for experts who sell coaching alongside courses.

**LearnWorlds** offers a community platform with collections, spaces, and threaded course discussions. Students can create public profiles and connect with peers — useful for cohort-based programs. LearnWorlds also includes gamification features: badges, points, and leaderboards to incentivize course completion. A student profile directory lets learners browse and network with each other.

LearnWorlds lacks a dedicated coaching product, though. If one-on-one coaching is a significant part of your business, you'd need to manage it outside the platform or build a workaround with live sessions.

**Winner: Tie** — LearnWorlds edges ahead on gamification and peer networking. Teachable wins on coaching. Choose based on your product mix.

### Marketing & Sales Tools

**Teachable** covers the basics well: coupon codes, order bumps, upsells, email marketing (basic), and an affiliate program (Builder plan and above). Teachable Payments processes credit cards natively, and the platform auto-detects and collects sales tax — a meaningful convenience feature. Affiliate payouts are automated, reducing admin work.

However, Teachable lacks built-in funnel building, popups, and advanced email automation. Most users pair it with an external email platform (ConvertKit, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign) and potentially an external funnel builder.

**LearnWorlds** includes built-in sales funnels, popups, email capture forms, tag-based segmentation, and an affiliate program (Pro Trainer+). The platform supports subscriptions, bundles, and flexible payment structures natively. Email automation is more capable than Teachable's, though still not a replacement for dedicated email platforms.

One notable gap: LearnWorlds' affiliate payouts are manual, not automated. And for sales tax collection, you'll need to integrate a third-party tool like Quaderno.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — for built-in marketing capabilities. Teachable wins on payment convenience (automated affiliates, built-in tax collection).

### Analytics & Reporting

**Teachable** offers basic student progress tracking and sales reports across all plans. You can see enrollment numbers, revenue, completion rates, and individual student progress. It's functional but not deep — there's no engagement-level analytics, no video heatmaps, and limited data export options on lower plans.

**LearnWorlds** provides detailed learner analytics on its Learning Center plan, including video engagement tracking, quiz performance analysis, time-on-task metrics, and custom reports. For training businesses that need to demonstrate learning outcomes or compliance, this level of reporting matters. However, the advanced analytics are locked behind the $249/month plan — a significant investment.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — but only if you're on the Learning Center plan. At the Basic/Pro level, both platforms offer similar reporting depth.

### Integrations & Ecosystem

**Teachable** integrates natively with Zapier, Mailchimp, Google Analytics, ConvertKit, Segment, and several other tools. Zapier extends connectivity to thousands of apps. However, advanced integrations (webhooks, API access) are limited to higher-tier plans.

**LearnWorlds** offers native integrations with HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, Zoom, Zapier, Mailchimp, and others. It also provides webhooks and API access on the Learning Center plan, plus SSO (Single Sign-On) for enterprise deployments. The Zoom integration is particularly useful for live class delivery within courses.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — slightly, due to broader native integrations and SSO support. Both platforms work well with Zapier for extending connectivity.

### Mobile Experience

**Teachable** offers a generic mobile app (iOS and Android) where students can access courses on the go. The app is Teachable-branded — your school appears within the Teachable app alongside other schools. You can't customize the app's appearance or publish it independently.

**LearnWorlds** takes a different approach: on the Learning Center plan, you can build a white-label mobile app published under your own brand in the App Store and Google Play. The app supports in-app purchases, push notifications, and offline content access. This is a major advantage for training businesses that want a professional mobile presence.

**Winner: LearnWorlds** — the white-label app is a significant differentiator for businesses that need branded mobile delivery.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Teachable If You're An Expert Selling Courses & Coaching

You want to take your knowledge, package it into courses or coaching programs, and start selling with minimal friction. You're comfortable with basic design and don't need SCORM, interactive video, or complex learning paths. Teachable's coaching product is a genuine standout — if one-on-one or group coaching is part of your model, no other platform at this price point does it as natively.

**Best Teachable plan for experts:** Builder ($69/mo annual) — removes transaction fees, adds certificates and affiliates.

### Choose LearnWorlds If You're Running a Training Academy

You're building a training business with structured curricula, certifications, multiple instructors, and professional learning experiences. You need interactive content, SCORM compliance, branded mobile apps, and detailed learner analytics. LearnWorlds' toolkit is designed for this use case, and the investment in the Pro Trainer or Learning Center plan pays off in credibility and learning outcomes.

**Best LearnWorlds plan for academies:** Pro Trainer ($79/mo annual) to start; Learning Center ($249/mo annual) once you need white-labeling and advanced analytics.

### Choose LearnWorlds If You're a Company Adding Customer Training

You're adding an education layer to your product — onboarding courses, certification programs, or customer enablement content. SSO, API access, white-label branding, and SCORM support are likely requirements. LearnWorlds handles these at the Learning Center tier. Teachable doesn't offer SSO or SCORM at any price point, making it a poor fit for this use case.

**Best LearnWorlds plan for companies:** Learning Center ($249/mo annual) or High Volume/Corporate (custom pricing).

### When Neither Is the Right Fit

If you need a true all-in-one platform with deep email marketing, advanced funnel building, membership sites, and course hosting under one roof, neither Teachable nor LearnWorlds fully delivers. Both require external tools for complete marketing stacks. Platforms like Kajabi (higher price, more all-in-one) or Thinkific (comparable pricing, zero transaction fees on all plans, strong community features) may be worth evaluating depending on your needs.

## Verdict

Teachable and LearnWorlds target overlapping markets but serve different needs.

**Teachable is the better choice** for experts who want simplicity, speed, and a coaching-first business model. It removes friction from the launch process and handles payments cleanly. Its weakness is depth — if your courses need to be more than video-plus-quiz, you'll hit walls.

**LearnWorlds is the better choice** for academies, training businesses, and companies that need professional learning tools. Interactive video, SCORM, white-label apps, and structured assessments set it apart. Its weakness is complexity and cost — you need the $79–$249/month plans to unlock the features that justify choosing it over simpler alternatives.

**The decision often comes down to one question:** Is your primary product *content you sell* (Teachable) or *learning experiences you deliver* (LearnWorlds)?

If you're selling a $97 course to your email list, Teachable gets you there faster. If you're delivering a $500 certification program with assessments, compliance tracking, and branded mobile access, LearnWorlds is purpose-built for that.

Neither platform is a bad choice — but picking the wrong one for your use case means either paying for features you don't use or running into limitations that force a migration later.
