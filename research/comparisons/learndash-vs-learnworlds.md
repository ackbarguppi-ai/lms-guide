# LearnDash vs LearnWorlds: WordPress Plugin vs Hosted Platform

**The short answer:** LearnDash is the better choice if you already run WordPress and want maximum customization, deep quiz logic, and control over your infrastructure. LearnWorlds is the better choice if you want a fully hosted platform with standout interactive video, built-in marketing tools, and zero server management. Neither is universally superior — the right pick depends on your technical comfort, team size, and what kind of learning experience you're building.

LearnDash is a WordPress plugin that turns any WordPress site into a full LMS. LearnWorlds is a standalone hosted platform — sign up, build, launch. That architectural difference shapes everything else: pricing, flexibility, maintenance burden, and who each platform actually serves well.

This comparison breaks down both platforms across the categories that matter most, with current pricing and specific feature details so you can make an informed decision.

## Pricing Breakdown

### LearnDash Pricing (2026)

LearnDash offers two paths: a self-hosted WordPress plugin or a managed cloud platform.

**Self-Hosted Plugin:**

| Plan | Price | Sites |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Site | $199/yr | 1 |
| 10 Sites | $399/yr | Up to 10 |
| Unlimited Sites | $799/yr | Unlimited |

The plugin price is just the starting point. A realistic professional setup typically costs **$646–$1,396/yr** once you add essential extras:

- ProPanel (analytics): $199/yr
- Instructor Role: $99/yr
- Gradebook: $149/yr
- Groups Management: $199/yr
- Managed WordPress hosting: $300–$720/yr

The Professional Suite bundle ($799/yr) includes 11 of 12 add-ons at roughly 43% off individual pricing.

**LearnDash Cloud (Managed):**

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per mo) | Storage | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Essential | $39/mo | $29/mo | 15 GB | Non-technical users, small course catalogs |
| Plus | $69/mo | $55/mo | 30 GB | Growing operations needing analytics |
| Ultimate | $99/mo | $79/mo | 100 GB | Large catalogs, high-traffic programs |

Cloud plans include hosting, SSL, CDN, daily backups, and automatic updates. The Plus tier adds ProPanel analytics and multi-instructor support — features that cost extra on self-hosted.

### LearnWorlds Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per mo) | Transaction Fees | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $29/mo | $24/mo | **$5 per enrollment** | 3-page website, paid courses only |
| Pro Trainer | $99/mo | $79/mo | 0% | 20 SCORM limit |
| Learning Center | $295/mo | $249/mo | 0% | 25 admin cap |
| High Volume & Corporate | Custom | Custom | 0% | Contact sales |

**Watch out for the Starter plan.** The $5-per-sale fee adds up fast. Selling 14+ courses per month makes Pro Trainer cheaper. Selling 50 courses at $50 each means $250/mo in fees — nearly 9x the base plan cost. Starter also limits you to 3 website pages and doesn't allow free courses (no lead magnets).

**The realistic entry point is Pro Trainer at $79/mo (annual).** That's where you get 0% transaction fees, payment plans, certificates, free courses, and unlimited website pages.

## Category Breakdown

### Course Creation & Learning Experience

**LearnDash** excels at structured, academic-style course design. Courses are organized into a hierarchy: courses → sections → lessons → topics → quizzes. The quiz engine is one of the most powerful in any LMS — 8+ question types, timed assessments, question banks, randomization, retake limits, conditional progression, and essay/open-ended grading. If your courses depend on rigorous assessment (compliance training, certifications, academic programs), LearnDash has a clear edge.

**LearnWorlds** leads with multimedia engagement. Its **interactive video editor** is a genuine differentiator — embed quizzes, clickable hotspots, branching paths, annotations, and calls-to-action directly inside video timelines. No other platform in this price range offers anything comparable. LearnWorlds also supports live sessions, SCORM packages (1.2, 2004, and xAPI), and a more visually polished course player out of the box.

**Verdict:** LearnDash for assessment-heavy, structured programs. LearnWorlds for media-rich, engagement-driven learning.

### Customization & Design

**LearnDash** gives you the entire WordPress ecosystem. Use any theme, any page builder (Elementor, Divi, Beaver Builder), any plugin. Need a fully custom checkout flow? Wire up WooCommerce. Want a membership site with community forums? Add BuddyBoss. Need a specific integration that doesn't exist? A developer can build it. The ceiling on customization is essentially limitless — but so is the complexity.

**LearnWorlds** includes a drag-and-drop website builder, customizable course player, branded landing pages, and sales funnels. You can adjust colors, layouts, and branding without code. White-labeling (removing LearnWorlds branding) requires the Learning Center plan at $249/mo. The builder is capable but finite — you're working within LearnWorlds' design system, not building from scratch.

**Verdict:** LearnDash for teams with developers or WordPress expertise. LearnWorlds for experts and small teams who want good-looking results without touching code.

### Marketing & Sales

**LearnWorlds** has a clear advantage here. Built-in tools include:
- Sales pages and landing page builder
- Coupon and discount management
- Affiliate management
- SEO-optimized pages
- Bundling and subscription/membership options
- Email marketing integrations
- Conversion funnels

**LearnDash** relies on the WordPress ecosystem for marketing. WooCommerce handles e-commerce, and you'll add plugins for landing pages, SEO (Yoast/RankMath), email capture, and affiliate tracking. This can be more powerful in the end — WooCommerce alone is extraordinarily flexible — but requires assembling and maintaining multiple pieces.

**Verdict:** LearnWorlds for an integrated marketing stack out of the box. LearnDash if you want to build a custom marketing machine on WordPress (and have the skills or budget to do so).

### Community & Social Learning

**LearnWorlds** offers built-in community features: discussion forums, social learning elements, and learner networking. It's not as deep as a dedicated community platform, but it's included and works.

**LearnDash** has no native community. The most common solution is pairing it with **BuddyBoss** ($228/yr+), which creates a polished social learning environment with activity feeds, groups, messaging, and forums. BuddyBoss + LearnDash is one of the most popular combinations in the WordPress LMS space — but it's an additional cost and integration to manage.

**Verdict:** LearnWorlds for built-in simplicity. LearnDash + BuddyBoss for a richer community experience (at added cost and complexity).

### Integrations & Ecosystem

**LearnDash** wins on sheer integration breadth. WordPress connects to virtually everything. Native integrations include WooCommerce, Stripe, PayPal, Zapier, and most email marketing platforms. The WordPress plugin ecosystem adds thousands more options. Enterprise integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) are possible via WP Fusion ($247–$397/yr) plus custom development.

**LearnWorlds** integrates with major payment gateways, email marketing services (Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, ConvertKit), analytics platforms, Zapier, and CRM tools. Integrations are managed within the platform, reducing compatibility risks. API access is available on the Learning Center plan and above.

**Verdict:** LearnDash for organizations with complex existing tech stacks. LearnWorlds for straightforward integration needs.

### Setup, Maintenance & Technical Burden

This is where the architectural difference matters most.

**LearnWorlds:** Sign up → build course → publish. Hosting, SSL, updates, backups, and security are handled for you. No servers to manage, no plugins to keep compatible, no WordPress core updates to test. Ongoing technical burden: near zero.

**LearnDash (self-hosted):** Choose a host → install WordPress → install LearnDash → configure theme → install add-ons → set up payment gateway → test → launch. Ongoing: manage WordPress updates, plugin compatibility, hosting performance, security monitoring, and backups. Budget 10–20 hours/yr minimum for maintenance. A staging environment is recommended for testing updates.

**LearnDash Cloud:** Closer to the LearnWorlds experience — managed hosting, automatic updates, infrastructure handled. But customization is more limited than self-hosted, and you're still within the WordPress paradigm.

**Verdict:** LearnWorlds (or LearnDash Cloud) for minimal maintenance. Self-hosted LearnDash only if you have technical resources.

### Reporting & Analytics

**LearnDash** offers solid reporting through ProPanel ($199/yr add-on, included in Cloud Plus+): learner progress, quiz performance, course completion rates, and group reporting. The WordPress ecosystem adds Google Analytics integration and custom reporting via plugins or direct database access.

**LearnWorlds** includes built-in analytics on all plans, with more advanced reporting on higher tiers. Track course progress, learner engagement, revenue, and conversion metrics. The Learning Center plan adds advanced analytics with deeper insights.

**Verdict:** Roughly comparable. LearnDash's reporting requires ProPanel (extra cost on self-hosted). LearnWorlds includes analytics by default but gates advanced features behind higher tiers.

### Mobile Experience

**LearnDash** doesn't offer a native mobile app in its standard plans. Learners use responsive web views. A custom branded mobile app is available as a professional service ($1,999 setup).

**LearnWorlds** offers a branded mobile app on its High Volume & Corporate plan. Lower tiers use responsive web. The mobile experience is generally well-reviewed.

**Verdict:** Neither platform leads strongly on mobile unless you're on enterprise tiers. Both work on mobile browsers.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose LearnDash If You…

- **Already run a WordPress site** and want to add course capabilities without switching platforms
- **Need deep quiz and assessment logic** for certifications, compliance, or academic programs
- **Have developers or WordPress expertise** on your team (or budget for it)
- **Want maximum customization** — custom themes, unique checkout flows, specific integrations
- **Run multiple sites** — the Unlimited Sites license ($799/yr) is extremely cost-effective at scale
- **Need enterprise integrations** with tools like Salesforce, HubSpot, or custom SSO
- **Prefer to own your infrastructure** — full data control, no platform dependency

### Choose LearnWorlds If You…

- **Want to launch fast** without worrying about hosting, security, or WordPress management
- **Prioritize interactive, media-rich learning** — the interactive video editor is unmatched at this price point
- **Are an expert selling courses** who needs built-in marketing tools (funnels, affiliates, SEO pages) without assembling plugins
- **Need SCORM compliance** — supported on Pro Trainer and above
- **Don't have a technical team** and want a single vendor handling infrastructure
- **Want built-in community features** without adding third-party tools
- **Prefer a 30-day free trial** to test before committing

### Choose Neither — Consider Alternatives If You…

- **Need simplicity above all else** and don't require advanced features → look at Thinkific or Teachable
- **Want a free/open-source solution** → consider Moodle or Open edX
- **Need enterprise-grade corporate training** with compliance tracking → evaluate TalentLMS, Docebo, or Absorb

## Verdict

**For WordPress-native teams building structured, assessment-heavy programs:** LearnDash is the stronger choice. The quiz engine is best-in-class, the WordPress ecosystem gives you infinite flexibility, and the Unlimited Sites license makes it economical for agencies and multi-brand organizations. Just budget realistically — plan for $600–$2,300/yr depending on your setup, not the $199 sticker price.

**For experts and training companies who want to launch and sell quickly:** LearnWorlds wins on speed-to-market and built-in tools. The interactive video editor alone justifies consideration. Skip the Starter plan and go straight to Pro Trainer ($79/mo annual) — it's the real entry point. The all-in-one approach means fewer moving parts, fewer things to break, and more time spent on content instead of infrastructure.

**If you're genuinely torn:** LearnWorlds' 30-day free trial makes it the lower-risk starting point. You can test the full platform without spending anything. LearnDash requires a purchase commitment (though the 30-day refund policy provides a safety net). Start with LearnWorlds, and if you hit its customization ceiling, LearnDash will be there — it's not going anywhere.

Both are mature, capable platforms. The difference isn't quality — it's architecture. Hosted vs. self-managed. Convenience vs. control. Pick the tradeoff that matches your team.
