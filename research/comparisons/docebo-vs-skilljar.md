# Docebo vs Skilljar: Enterprise LMS vs Purpose-Built Customer Education

Docebo and Skilljar both help companies train customers and partners — but they come at the problem from opposite directions. Docebo is a full-spectrum enterprise LMS that handles employee training, customer education, and partner enablement from one platform. Skilljar (now a Gainsight product) is laser-focused on external training: customer onboarding, product adoption, and partner certification.

This comparison breaks down where each platform wins, where it falls short, and which one makes sense for your situation — whether you're an expert building a training business, an academy scaling professional development, or a company launching customer education alongside internal L&D.

**Bottom line:** Skilljar is the better choice if your primary need is customer and partner education with tight CRM integration and fast time-to-value. Docebo is the better choice if you need a single platform for employees, customers, and partners — and have the budget and IT resources for a more complex deployment.

## Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

Neither platform publishes transparent pricing. Both require sales conversations. Here's what the data shows.

### Docebo Pricing

Docebo uses a two-tier structure: **Elevate** (mid-market entry) and **Enterprise** (full-featured).

- **Starting price:** ~$25,000/year (Engage tier referenced at this level in reviews)
- **Average contract value:** $40,288/year across 79 deals tracked by Vendr
- **Verified enterprise quote:** $70,000/year for 1,000 users = $5.83/user/month
- **Estimated per-user cost:** $5–$10/month depending on scale and tier
- **Average negotiated savings:** 15.4% (Vendr data)
- **Minimum contract:** 1 year; enterprise deals typically 3–5 years

**Key cost factors:**
- Elevate tier includes up to 4 integrations; Enterprise includes 6
- Advanced Analytics and Branded Mobile App are Enterprise-only — no add-on path from Elevate
- eCommerce, Gamification, and Immersive Learning are paid add-ons on both tiers
- AI features run on a consumption-based credit system (launched January 2026): voice coaching at 55 credits/session, video presenter at 210 credits/minute, course creation at 10 credits/lesson
- Implementation included: 8-week structured onboarding with migration support for up to 100,000 records

**Watch out for:** The AI credit system creates variable costs. Heavy-usage organizations can consume 165,000+ credits annually, and the platform auto-invoices for overages. Budget 20–30% above projected AI usage.

### Skilljar Pricing

Skilljar uses a three-tier structure: **Essential**, **Professional**, and **Enterprise**.

- **Essential tier:** ~$30,000/year for 1,000 MAUs (list price per Vendr)
- **Professional tier:** ~$85,800/year for 10,000 MAUs (list, negotiable to $52,000–$62,000)
- **Enterprise tier:** Custom pricing for high-volume deployments
- **Pricing model:** Annual subscription + monthly active user (MAU) fees
- **Negotiated savings:** 9–32% on Essential; 18–39% on Professional (Vendr data)
- **Implementation costs:** $3,000–$5,000 (small), $5,000–$15,000 (mid-size), $20,000+ (enterprise)

**Key cost factors:**
- Essential includes 1 academy site; Professional includes 2; Enterprise includes 3
- Content Syndication add-on: $10,000
- Data Connector add-on: starting at $25,000
- Language Packs: starting at $10,000 (5 languages)
- Per-MAU costs drop significantly at volume: $30.10/MAU at 1,000 users → $10.10/MAU at 5,000 users (list), further reducible through negotiation
- Multi-year contracts unlock 30–42% discounts vs. 15–25% on annual deals

**Watch out for:** Add-on costs stack up fast. A Professional deployment with Data Connector and Language Packs can push total cost to $120,000+ before negotiation. Understand MAU counting methodology — it determines your bill.

### Pricing Verdict

Docebo's all-in-one approach can be more cost-effective if you're already paying for employee training and want to add customer education on the same platform. Skilljar can be more expensive per-feature for pure customer education, but you avoid paying for capabilities you don't need (like employee training modules). For a 1,000-user external training deployment, expect to pay $30,000–$50,000/year for either platform after negotiation.

## Category Breakdown

### 1. Customer and Partner Education

**Winner: Skilljar**

This is Skilljar's entire reason for existing. The platform was purpose-built to help SaaS companies, tech firms, and service organizations educate customers and certify partners.

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Purpose-built for external audiences — every feature is designed around customer education workflows
- Deep Salesforce integration maps training activity directly to customer health scores, renewal rates, and expansion revenue
- Built-in eCommerce supports subscriptions, course bundles, training credits, and monetization out of the box
- Certification with LinkedIn integration lets learners display credentials publicly
- Seamless Zoom and Webex integration for virtual instructor-led training (VILT)
- Since the 2025 Gainsight acquisition, tighter integration with customer success workflows is expected

**Docebo strengths:**
- Extended Enterprise module supports external audiences, but it's an add-on rather than the core product
- More advanced content creation tools with AI-powered authoring
- Stronger gamification features (points, badges, leaderboards)
- Social learning through Coach & Share
- Better suited when customer training is one of several audiences on a single platform

**The gap:** Skilljar treats customer education as a first-class citizen. Docebo treats it as one of several use cases — capable but not purpose-built. If customer education is your primary initiative, Skilljar's architecture gives you a more natural fit.

### 2. Employee Training and Internal L&D

**Winner: Docebo**

Skilljar explicitly does not compete here. If you need employee onboarding, compliance training, skill development, or internal L&D — Docebo is the only option of the two.

**Docebo strengths:**
- Full internal training capabilities: onboarding, compliance, skill development, leadership training
- AI-powered skill mapping and personalized learning paths via Harmony AI
- Social learning, gamification, and collaborative features for employee engagement
- Advanced Analytics (Enterprise tier) for executive reporting on training ROI
- Proven at scale: 17,000+ organizations including Brinkmann Constructors (90%+ mobile adoption), ChenMed (63% reduction in support tickets)

**Skilljar:** Not applicable. Skilljar is designed for external training only. If you need both internal and external training, you'll either need Docebo alone or Skilljar paired with a separate internal LMS.

### 3. Content Creation and Course Management

**Winner: Docebo**

Docebo has invested heavily in AI-powered content creation. Skilljar takes a more basic approach and expects you to bring content created elsewhere.

**Docebo strengths:**
- AI-assisted course creation (10 credits/lesson) — generate lessons, quizzes, and assessments
- AI Video Presenter creates AI avatar videos (210 credits/minute)
- AI Virtual Coaching for interactive skills practice (voice and chat modes)
- Built-in text refinement, translation, and quiz generation
- Social learning features let employees and customers contribute content

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Low-code page builder for straightforward course construction
- SCORM and xAPI support for importing content from authoring tools
- Virtual instructor-led training with integrated scheduling
- LearnExpert integration for AI-powered course creation (custom pricing add-on)
- Simpler interface — less powerful but faster to get started

**The gap:** Docebo's content tools are significantly more advanced, but they come with AI credit costs and complexity. Skilljar assumes you'll create content in tools like Articulate, Camtasia, or similar — then host and deliver through Skilljar. For teams with existing content workflows, Skilljar's approach is perfectly adequate. For teams that want AI-assisted creation in-platform, Docebo leads.

### 4. Integrations and Tech Stack Fit

**Winner: Depends on your stack**

Both platforms integrate with major enterprise tools, but their integration philosophies differ.

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Salesforce-native integration — maps training completions to accounts, contacts, and opportunities
- Deep customer success platform connectivity (especially with Gainsight post-acquisition)
- Google Analytics integration for tracking learner behavior
- REST API access on all tiers
- Content syndication to external LMS platforms (Professional+)
- Focused integration set — fewer total connectors but deeper where it counts for customer education

**Docebo strengths:**
- 400+ integration connectors (marketplace)
- Salesforce, Workday, SAP, Microsoft Teams, Slack, and more
- HRIS and HCM integrations for employee data sync
- SSO and SAML support across tiers
- Up to 4 integrations included (Elevate) or 6 (Enterprise); additional integrations require professional services

**The gap:** Skilljar's Salesforce integration is widely cited as best-in-class for customer education use cases — it connects training directly to revenue and retention metrics. Docebo offers broader integration breadth but reviews note that the "400+ integrations" claim overstates plug-and-play readiness; complex integrations often require premium partner support and API development. If your primary integration need is Salesforce + customer success tools, Skilljar wins. If you need HRIS, HCM, and broad enterprise connectivity, Docebo wins.

### 5. Analytics and Reporting

**Winner: Docebo (with caveats)**

**Docebo strengths:**
- Advanced Analytics (Enterprise tier only) provides executive-grade dashboards and custom reporting
- AI-driven insights through Harmony engine
- Compliance tracking, certification management, and audit trails
- Multi-audience reporting across employee, customer, and partner training

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Custom reporting and benchmarks included on all tiers
- Data Connector add-on ($25,000+) provides real-time PostgreSQL database access for advanced analytics
- Training activity maps to Salesforce for revenue attribution
- Simpler out-of-the-box reporting focused on customer education metrics: completion rates, certification status, time-to-competency

**The gap:** Docebo's analytics are more powerful but locked behind the Enterprise tier and noted by users as needing improvement for compliance tracking. Skilljar's analytics are simpler but more immediately useful for customer education — especially the ability to correlate training with Salesforce revenue data. Skilljar's Data Connector is expensive ($25,000+) but gives data teams direct database access for custom BI.

### 6. User Experience and Administration

**Winner: Skilljar**

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Cleaner, simpler admin interface — purpose-built for one use case means less complexity
- Faster implementation (as quick as 3 weeks for basic setups)
- White-labeled academy sites with custom themes on all tiers
- Intuitive learner experience with straightforward navigation

**Docebo strengths:**
- Polished learner-facing interface (consistently praised in reviews)
- Extensive customization and localization for global deployments
- Multilingual support and UI localization
- Branded mobile app (Enterprise tier)

**The gap:** Docebo's learner experience is excellent, but admin complexity is a consistent criticism. Users report needing "very deliberate upfront design work" and often premium partner support for setup. Skilljar is simpler to administer because it does less — but for customer education specifically, that simplicity is a feature. Docebo's admin complexity reflects its broader scope.

### 7. Scalability and Global Reach

**Winner: Docebo**

**Docebo strengths:**
- Proven at massive scale (17,000+ organizations)
- Multilingual support with AI-powered translation
- Global compliance and localization features
- Multi-audience architecture handles diverse user populations from one instance
- SOC 2 and ISO 27001 certified

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Scales well for external training (thousands of MAUs)
- Language Packs available ($10,000 for 5 languages)
- Multiple academy sites (up to 3 on Enterprise)
- Adequate for most mid-market global deployments

**The gap:** If you're training tens of thousands of users across dozens of countries in multiple languages, Docebo's infrastructure and localization are stronger. For most mid-market customer education programs serving hundreds to low thousands of external learners, Skilljar scales perfectly well.

### 8. Support Quality

**Winner: Skilljar**

**Skilljar strengths:**
- Purpose-built customer education vendor — support teams understand the use case deeply
- Gainsight acquisition brings customer success expertise
- Responsive support reported across review platforms

**Docebo concerns:**
- Support quality is the platform's most controversial area
- G2: 4.3/5 overall, but TrustRadius Customer Service: 1.9/10 — a dramatic gap
- Enterprise customers report significantly worse support than SMB customers
- Users report dependency on customer success managers for basic admin tasks like certification management
- Reviews recommend explicitly negotiating support SLAs before signing

**The gap:** Docebo's support inconsistency is well-documented. The 5.4-point gap between overall TrustRadius satisfaction (7.3/10) and Customer Service (1.9/10) is a red flag for enterprise buyers. Skilljar's narrower focus means support teams are more aligned with your specific needs.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Skilljar If:

- **Customer and partner education is your primary use case** — it's what the platform was built for
- **You're a SaaS company** tying training to product adoption, renewal rates, and expansion revenue
- **Salesforce is your CRM** — Skilljar's native integration is best-in-class for mapping training to revenue
- **You want faster time-to-value** — simpler setup, purpose-built workflows, less admin overhead
- **You don't need employee training** — Skilljar doesn't do internal L&D, and that's by design
- **You want built-in monetization** — subscriptions, bundles, and training credits out of the box
- **Your team is lean** — Skilljar requires less dedicated LMS administration than Docebo

**Typical Skilljar buyer:** SaaS company with 100–5,000 customers/partners to train, strong Salesforce usage, customer success team driving adoption metrics, no internal L&D requirement from this platform.

### Choose Docebo If:

- **You need one platform for everything** — employees, customers, partners, all in one place
- **AI-powered content creation matters** — Docebo's authoring tools are significantly more advanced
- **You're a large enterprise** with complex, multi-audience training requirements
- **Global deployment is critical** — Docebo's localization, translation, and compliance features are stronger
- **You have dedicated LMS admin resources** — Docebo rewards teams that can invest in configuration
- **Advanced analytics drive decisions** — Docebo's Enterprise-tier analytics are more powerful (though pricey)
- **You want a branded mobile app** — only available in Docebo's Enterprise tier

**Typical Docebo buyer:** Enterprise (1,000+ employees) with training needs spanning internal teams, customer onboarding, and partner certification, dedicated L&D team, IT resources for integration work, multi-year budget commitment.

### Consider Neither If:

- **You're an expert selling courses independently** — both platforms are enterprise-focused. Look at Thinkific, Teachable, or Kajabi instead.
- **You're a training academy** running cohort-based programs — consider platforms with stronger community and cohort features like Teachfloor or Thinkific.
- **Budget is under $25,000/year** — both platforms start above this threshold. TalentLMS, LearnDash, or Thinkific offer more affordable entry points.
- **You need a content authoring tool** — neither replaces Articulate, Rise, or Camtasia for serious course production.

## Verdict

Docebo and Skilljar aren't really competing for the same buyer — they're solving different problems with overlapping capabilities.

**Skilljar** is the specialist. If you're a SaaS or tech company whose primary need is making customers successful through education, Skilljar delivers a tighter, faster, more CRM-connected experience. The Gainsight acquisition in 2025 positions it to become even more integrated with customer success workflows. You'll pay $30,000–$85,000/year depending on scale, and you'll get a platform that does one thing exceptionally well.

**Docebo** is the generalist powerhouse. If you need to train employees, educate customers, and certify partners from a single platform — and you have the budget ($40,000–$200,000+/year) and IT resources to manage complexity — Docebo delivers unmatched breadth. Its AI features are genuinely innovative, and its multi-audience architecture is best-in-class. But the support quality concerns are real, the AI credit system adds cost unpredictability, and admin complexity means you need dedicated people managing the platform.

**The deciding question:** Is customer education your *only* training initiative, or one of several?

- If it's your only initiative → **Skilljar**
- If it's one of several → **Docebo**
- If you're not sure yet → Start with Skilljar (lower complexity, faster ROI) and evaluate Docebo when internal training needs emerge
