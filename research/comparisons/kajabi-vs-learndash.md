# Kajabi vs LearnDash (2026): All-in-One Platform or WordPress Control?

**Last updated:** March 2026

Kajabi and LearnDash represent two fundamentally different approaches to selling online courses. **Kajabi is a fully hosted, all-in-one business platform** — courses, email marketing, sales funnels, community, and website all live under one roof. **LearnDash is a WordPress LMS plugin** that turns any WordPress site into a full-featured learning management system, with deep course-building tools and the flexibility of the entire WordPress ecosystem.

The choice comes down to a core trade-off: **convenience and integration vs. control and cost.** Kajabi handles everything for you at a premium price. LearnDash costs less upfront but requires you to assemble your own tech stack — hosting, email, payments, and marketing tools — around WordPress.

This guide compares pricing, features, and real trade-offs so you can choose based on your business model — whether you're an expert selling courses, an academy running structured training, or a company adding customer education.

## Category-by-Category Breakdown

### 1. Course Building & Content Delivery

**Kajabi** provides a clean, intuitive course builder with video hosting, drip content, quizzes, and assignments. Cohort-based courses are available on the Growth plan and above. It handles video hosting natively — no need for a separate Vimeo or Wistia account. The course builder works well but isn't the platform's primary differentiator; the business system around the course is.

**LearnDash** is where course building really shines. It offers a drag-and-drop builder with deep structural control: courses contain sections (formerly lessons), topics, and quizzes in a hierarchical tree. LearnDash supports multiple learning models — self-paced, scheduled (drip), and cohort-based. Its Focus Mode strips away site distractions to keep students in the learning flow.

LearnDash also provides shared course steps (reuse lessons or quizzes across courses), prerequisite chains, and course points — features that matter for structured education programs. Its AI-powered course outline and quiz builder can accelerate course creation from a rough idea to a structured skeleton.

**Winner: LearnDash.** If course structure, learning paths, and student engagement tools are your priority, LearnDash has deeper capabilities. Kajabi's builder is cleaner and faster to use, but less powerful for complex curricula.

### 2. Quizzes & Assessments

**Kajabi** offers basic quizzes and surveys within courses. You can add multiple-choice questions, use assessments to gate progress, and collect survey responses. It works for simple knowledge checks and engagement, but it's not built for rigorous assessment.

**LearnDash** takes quizzes seriously. It supports eight question types (multiple choice, fill-in-the-blank, sorting, matching, essays, free text, and more), randomized question pools, time limits, retry restrictions, and certificate generation tied to quiz results. LearnDash quizzes can serve as prerequisites for course advancement, meeting compliance and certification requirements.

For academies running accredited programs or companies needing verifiable competency checks, LearnDash's quiz engine is in a different league.

**Winner: LearnDash** by a wide margin. Its assessment engine is built for educational rigor, not just engagement.

### 3. Marketing & Sales Tools

**Kajabi** is a marketing platform that happens to host courses. Built-in email marketing with automation sequences, visual pipeline builders, landing page templates, and sales funnels are included on every plan. The email system supports tags, segments, broadcasts, and multi-step automations. Kajabi's marketing tools rival standalone platforms like ConvertKit or Mailchimp.

Sales pages, checkout flows, upsells, and order bumps are native. The affiliate program (Growth plan and above) lets partners promote your courses for commissions — all managed within Kajabi.

**LearnDash** has no native marketing tools. Email marketing, landing pages, and sales funnels all require third-party integrations. You'll connect Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or ConvertKit via WP Fusion or Zapier. For funnels and landing pages, you'll add Elementor Pro, CartFlows, or ClickFunnels.

The WordPress ecosystem offers enormous flexibility — you can plug in nearly any marketing tool — but you're responsible for selecting, configuring, and maintaining each integration. That's a real time cost.

**Winner: Kajabi** decisively. For marketing-savvy experts who want everything in one dashboard, Kajabi eliminates the integration tax entirely.

### 4. Payments & Monetization

**Kajabi** handles payments natively through Kajabi Payments (powered by Stripe). It supports one-time purchases, subscriptions, payment plans, free trials, and coupon codes. Checkout pages are customizable with order bumps and upsells. The affiliate program manages partner payouts automatically.

Kajabi charges no platform transaction fees but does apply payment processing fees (2.7%–2.9% + $0.30 depending on plan). Using your own Stripe account incurs an additional surcharge.

**LearnDash** relies on integrations for payment processing. The plugin includes native Stripe and PayPal support for one-time purchases, subscriptions, memberships, and course bundles. For more advanced e-commerce (cart functionality, tax handling, invoices), you'll add WooCommerce — which is free but adds complexity.

LearnDash charges zero platform fees. Payment processor fees are whatever Stripe or PayPal charge directly (typically 2.9% + $0.30), with no markup.

**Winner: Tie.** Both handle core payment needs well. Kajabi wins on convenience and built-in upsells/affiliates. LearnDash wins on flexibility and lower total processing costs (no platform surcharge).

### 5. Community & Student Engagement

**Kajabi** includes a built-in community feature on all plans (1 community on Basic, 3 on Pro). Members can post, comment, and interact in a feed-style layout. It's functional — similar to a basic Facebook Group — but not deeply featured. There's no robust forum structure, private messaging between members, or advanced moderation tools.

**LearnDash** doesn't include community features natively, but integrates with BuddyBoss and bbPress for fully featured community experiences. BuddyBoss in particular offers social networking profiles, activity feeds, groups, forums, private messaging, and deep LMS integration. The BuddyBoss + LearnDash combination is one of the most popular community-powered learning setups in the WordPress ecosystem.

LearnDash also has native gamification: badges, certificates, course points, and leaderboards. These features drive engagement in ways that Kajabi's simpler community doesn't match.

**Winner: LearnDash** (with BuddyBoss). The integration adds cost and setup time, but the result is a far richer community experience than Kajabi offers natively.

### 6. Website & Design

**Kajabi** includes a full website builder with professionally designed templates. Your website, blog, landing pages, and course pages all live on the same platform with consistent branding. No coding required. The design options are polished but somewhat constrained — you're working within Kajabi's template system.

**LearnDash** inherits the full design power of WordPress. With thousands of themes and page builders (Elementor, Divi, Kadence, GeneratePress), you have virtually unlimited design control. LearnDash's Focus Mode provides a distraction-free learning interface that can be customized to match your brand.

The trade-off: WordPress design freedom requires more effort. Picking a theme, configuring a page builder, and ensuring everything works together takes time (or money, if you hire a developer).

**Winner: Depends.** Kajabi wins for speed and polish out of the box. LearnDash/WordPress wins for design control and uniqueness — if you're willing to invest the setup effort.

### 7. Scalability & Technical Control

**Kajabi** handles hosting, security, updates, and performance. You don't touch servers. This is a genuine advantage for experts who want to focus on content and marketing, not infrastructure. The downside: you're subject to Kajabi's limits (contact caps, product limits) and can't modify the platform's underlying code.

**LearnDash** on WordPress gives you full technical control. You own the data, choose your hosting, and can modify anything with custom code or plugins. This matters for companies with compliance requirements, custom integrations, or non-standard workflows.

For large-scale deployments (thousands of concurrent students), LearnDash on a well-configured server (or managed WordPress host like WP Engine or Cloudways) can handle serious traffic. But performance tuning is your responsibility.

**Winner: LearnDash** for technical control and ownership. **Kajabi** for zero-maintenance simplicity.

### 8. Mobile Experience

**Kajabi** offers a branded mobile app for students on the Pro plan ($399/mo annual). On lower plans, students access courses through the mobile browser, which is responsive but not native. The branded app provides push notifications, offline access, and a native feel.

**LearnDash** offers a dedicated mobile app ($1,199/year) that syncs with your WordPress LMS. It supports offline learning, cross-device progress tracking, certificates, and drip content. The app requires a done-for-you setup service and app store submission, adding both cost and lead time (3–4 weeks).

**Winner: Tie.** Both offer mobile apps at premium price points. Kajabi's is more accessible (bundled with Pro). LearnDash's is a separate, significant investment.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Kajabi If You Are…

**An expert building a personal brand business.** You sell courses, coaching, and digital products. Marketing is central to your business — you run email sequences, build funnels, and optimize checkout flows. You want everything in one place and you're willing to pay a premium to avoid stitching together multiple tools. You'd rather spend time creating content and marketing than managing WordPress.

**A small team launching a membership or coaching program.** You need a landing page, a checkout flow, email onboarding, course delivery, and a community — and you need it all working together without hiring a developer.

**Someone who values simplicity over control.** You don't want to think about hosting, plugin updates, or security patches. You want a platform that just works.

### Choose LearnDash If You Are…

**An academy or training organization with structured curricula.** You need prerequisite chains, multiple learning modes, rigorous assessments, certificates, and compliance tracking. Your courses are more educational institution than marketing funnel. LearnDash's course architecture handles complex programs that Kajabi can't match.

**A company adding customer or employee training.** You need LMS features like group management, instructor roles, gradebooks, and SCORM/xAPI support. You may need to integrate with existing enterprise tools (HR systems, CRMs). WordPress gives you the API access and plugin flexibility to connect everything.

**A WordPress expert (or team with one).** You already run WordPress. You're comfortable with plugins, themes, and hosting. You want to add LMS functionality to your existing site without migrating to a walled garden. You prefer owning your tech stack and data.

**A budget-conscious expert willing to do the work.** You're starting out and $143/mo for Kajabi doesn't make sense yet. You can set up WordPress, install LearnDash, connect Mailchimp and Stripe, and build your own landing pages. You'll spend more time on setup but less money monthly.

### Neither Is Ideal If You…

**Need enterprise-grade LMS features out of the box** (SCORM authoring, advanced analytics, multi-tenant architecture). Look at Docebo, Absorb, or TalentLMS.

**Want a simple, low-cost course storefront.** Look at Teachable, Podia, or Thinkific — they're simpler than LearnDash and cheaper than Kajabi.

**Need to sell courses on a marketplace.** Neither platform is a marketplace. Look at Udemy or Skillshare for discoverability (with trade-offs on pricing control and margins).

## Verdict

**Kajabi is the better choice for marketing-driven experts** who sell courses, coaching, and memberships as part of a personal brand business. If your business model is "build audience → nurture with email → convert with funnels → deliver courses," Kajabi does all of that in one platform. You'll pay $143–$399/mo, but you'll replace $100–$200/mo in separate marketing tools and save significant time on integration headaches.

**LearnDash is the better choice for education-first organizations** — academies, training companies, and WordPress-native businesses that need deep course structure, rigorous assessments, and technical control. The plugin costs $199/year. Your total spend depends on the tools you add around it, but you'll typically spend less than Kajabi while gaining more flexibility.

Neither platform is objectively "better." They serve different business models. The expert who needs a marketing machine and the academy that needs an educational backbone are solving fundamentally different problems — and each platform is purpose-built for one of those problems.

**Bottom line:** If you're choosing between these two, the answer is probably obvious from your business model. If it's not, start with the question: *"Am I primarily a marketer who teaches, or an educator who markets?"* Kajabi is for the first. LearnDash is for the second.
