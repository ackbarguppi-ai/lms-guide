# Thinkific vs Skilljar: Course Platform vs Purpose-Built Customer Education

Thinkific and Skilljar both help you deliver training, but they serve fundamentally different markets. **Thinkific is a course platform** — built for experts, academies, and companies who need to create and sell online courses with flexible pricing and strong learning tools. **Skilljar is a purpose-built customer education platform** — designed specifically for SaaS companies and enterprises that need to onboard customers, reduce churn, and tie training directly to revenue metrics.

The right choice depends on who you're training and what you're optimizing for. If you're an expert or academy selling courses to individuals, Thinkific is purpose-built for your business model. If you're a B2B SaaS company training customers at scale with Salesforce integration requirements, Skilljar is the specialist tool.

Here's how they compare across every category that matters.

## Course Building and Content Management

### Where Thinkific leads:
- **Built-in course authoring** — drag-and-drop builder with AI assistance for quiz generation
- **Multiple content types** — video, text, audio, PDFs, embeds, downloads, live lessons
- **AI quiz generator** — creates questions from your course content automatically
- **Unlimited courses on every plan** — no artificial caps on content volume
- **Drip scheduling and prerequisite locking** — control content access by date or completion
- **Completion certificates** — design and issue branded certificates
- **Assignments and exams** — student submissions with instructor grading

### Where Skilljar leads:
- **SCORM/xAPI support** — import content from Articulate, Captivate, and other authoring tools
- **Learning paths** — structured curricula with sequenced courses and certifications
- **Certification with LinkedIn integration** — learners display credentials on their profiles
- **Virtual instructor-led training (VILT)** — seamless Zoom and Webex integration
- **Content syndication** — distribute courses to external LMS platforms (Professional+)
- **Multi-academy management** — up to 3 branded academy sites (Enterprise)

### Key difference:
Thinkific expects you to build courses in-platform. Skilljar expects you to build courses elsewhere (Articulate, Rise, Camtasia) and import them. Thinkific is the authoring tool + delivery platform; Skilljar is the delivery platform for content created elsewhere.

## Customer Education and Training Use Cases

This is where the platforms diverge most dramatically.

### Thinkific for customer education:
Thinkific works for customer education when:
- You're a smaller company with hundreds (not thousands) of customers to train
- You don't need deep CRM integration
- You want to monetize training directly
- Your customer success team is small

**How companies use Thinkific for customer training:**
- Create onboarding courses for new customers
- Build certification programs for power users
- Offer paid training as a revenue stream
- Deliver product tutorials and feature walkthroughs

**Limitations for customer education:**
- No native Salesforce integration (requires third-party tools)
- No built-in customer health score correlation
- Manual learner management at scale
- No customer success platform connectivity

### Skilljar for customer education:
Skilljar is purpose-built for customer education:
- **Salesforce-native integration** — training completions map to accounts, contacts, opportunities
- **Customer health correlation** — tie training data to retention, expansion, and NPS
- **Automated learner management** — rules-based enrollment and progression
- **Customer success platform integration** — Gainsight, Zendesk, and more
- **Onboarding acceleration** — structured paths from signup to product expertise
- **Reduced support costs** — self-service training deflects tickets

**Skilljar customer results:**
- Asana: +19 point NPS improvement
- Smartsheet: $1M+ in training revenue first year
- Egnyte: 2x faster time-to-value

### Verdict: Customer Education
**Skilljar wins decisively** for dedicated customer education at scale. The Salesforce integration, customer health correlation, and purpose-built workflows are why enterprise SaaS companies pay 10–20x more for Skilljar. Thinkific can work for customer education in smaller organizations or when training is a revenue center rather than a retention tool.

## Who Should Choose Thinkific

### Experts selling courses independently
You create content, market it yourself, and sell to individual learners. Thinkific's $49/mo entry point and built-in course authoring make it the obvious choice.

**Best plan:** Start ($99/mo) for memberships, certificates, and full customization.

### Academies running training businesses
You're building a business around professional development. Thinkific's unlimited courses, flexible pricing, and strong eCommerce tools support your growth.

**Best plan:** Grow ($199/mo) for API access, advanced analytics, and affiliate tools.

### Small to mid-size companies adding customer education
You have hundreds (not thousands) of customers and want to offer training without a massive budget. Thinkific Plus can handle SSO and CRM integration at a fraction of Skilljar's cost.

**Best plan:** Plus (custom pricing, typically $10–20K/year).

### Companies monetizing training
Training is a revenue center. You sell certifications, courses, and memberships to customers and partners. Thinkific's eCommerce flexibility and 0% fees maximize your margins.

**Best plan:** Grow or Plus depending on scale.

## Who Should Choose Skilljar

### SaaS companies with 1,000+ customers
You're scaling customer education and need to prove ROI. Skilljar's Salesforce integration lets you correlate training with retention, expansion, and revenue.

**Best plan:** Professional (~$50–85K/year) for multiple academies and premium integrations.

### Companies with dedicated customer education budget
You have $50,000+ annually allocated to customer training and see it as a strategic retention/expansion tool, not a cost center.

**Best plan:** Professional or Enterprise depending on MAU volume.

### Organizations requiring Salesforce-native integration
Your customer success workflow runs on Salesforce. Skilljar's native integration is best-in-class for mapping training to customer health scores and opportunities.

**Best plan:** Professional or Enterprise.

### Companies reducing support costs through self-service
You measure success by support ticket deflection and time-to-value. Skilljar's analytics tie training directly to these metrics.

**Best plan:** Essentials or Professional depending on scale.

## Who Should NOT Choose Each Platform

### Don't choose Thinkific if:
- You need native Salesforce integration with training-to-revenue attribution
- You're training 5,000+ customers and need automated learner management at scale
- You have a dedicated customer education budget of $50,000+ and want enterprise support
- You need SCORM/xAPI compliance for importing content from enterprise authoring tools (only on Plus plan)

### Don't choose Skilljar if:
- You're an individual creator or small academy — the $30,000 minimum is cost-prohibitive
- You need built-in course authoring tools — Skilljar expects imported content
- You want to launch quickly without implementation support — the 3–8 week setup is required
- You need a branded mobile app for learners — Skilljar has no native app option
- You're training employees internally — Skilljar is external-only

---

## Final Verdict

**Choose Thinkific** if you're an expert, academy, or company building a training business. It's affordable, flexible, and has the course authoring and eCommerce tools you need to sell and deliver training. The pricing scales from $49/mo to enterprise levels without requiring massive upfront commitments.

**Choose Skilljar** if you're a SaaS or enterprise company with dedicated customer education needs, Salesforce integration requirements, and budget to match. The 10–20x price premium is justified when training is tied directly to customer retention, expansion, and revenue metrics.

**The deciding question:** Are you selling training as a product, or is training a retention/expansion tool for your existing product?

- If training = product → **Thinkific**
- If training = customer success tool → **Skilljar**

Both platforms are mature and well-supported in their respective markets. They're not really competing for the same buyer — they're solving different problems at different scales and price points.
