# Kajabi vs Teachable (2026): Which Platform Fits Your Business?

**Last updated:** March 2026

Kajabi and Teachable are two of the most popular platforms for selling online courses, coaching, and digital products — but they serve different needs. **Kajabi is an all-in-one business platform** with built-in email marketing, sales funnels, and landing pages. **Teachable is a focused course platform** with lower entry pricing and strong payment infrastructure.

This guide breaks down pricing, features, and real trade-offs so you can choose based on your actual business model — whether you're an expert selling courses, an academy running a training business, or a company adding customer education.

## Category-by-Category Breakdown

### 1. Course Building & Content Delivery

**Kajabi** offers a solid course builder with video hosting, drip content, quizzes, and assignments. Cohort-based courses (time-released group programs) are available on the Growth plan ($199/mo annual) and above. The course builder is functional but not the platform's primary strength — Kajabi's focus is the business system around the course.

**Teachable** has a more refined course builder with a cleaner student experience. Features include auto-graded quizzes, short-answer assessments, course compliance tracking, drip content, and certificates on all plans. Teachable also provides AI-powered course creation tools, free subtitles and translations, and up to 1TB of video storage (2TB on Advanced).

**Winner: Teachable.** More course-specific features at lower price points, better student experience out of the box, and useful extras like auto-translations and compliance tracking.

### 2. Marketing & Sales Tools

**Kajabi** is where the marketing gap becomes a canyon. Built-in features include:
- **Email marketing** with sequences, broadcasts, and automations
- **Sales funnels** with pre-built templates
- **Landing pages** with a drag-and-drop builder
- **Checkout pages** with one-click upsells
- **Advanced automations** (Growth plan+) that trigger actions based on student behavior
- **Comment-to-DM** functionality for social engagement

These tools replace separate subscriptions to email platforms (ConvertKit, Mailchimp) and funnel builders (ClickFunnels, Leadpages).

**Teachable** offers basic marketing tools:
- **Built-in email marketing** on all plans
- **Upsells and cart recovery** (abandoned cart emails)
- **Coupons and bundles**
- **Student referral program**

But Teachable lacks built-in sales funnels and advanced landing page builders. Most Teachable users pair the platform with external tools like ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or ClickFunnels for full marketing capability.

**Winner: Kajabi, decisively.** If marketing automation, funnels, and landing pages matter to your business, Kajabi's built-in toolkit is genuinely valuable. However, if you already have a marketing stack you like, this advantage diminishes.

### 3. Website & Design

**Kajabi** includes a full website builder with professional templates, custom domains, and blog functionality. You can run your entire web presence from Kajabi — no separate WordPress or Squarespace site needed. The page builder produces polished, modern designs. Pro plan users get up to 3 websites.

**Teachable** provides a school website with customizable themes, custom domains, and CSS snippets for design tweaks. It's functional for a course storefront but not a replacement for a full business website. You'll likely still need a separate website for your broader online presence.

**Winner: Kajabi.** If you want one platform for everything — including your main website — Kajabi delivers. Teachable's site builder is adequate for a course storefront but limited beyond that.

### 4. Student Experience & Mobile

**Kajabi** provides the Kajabi Customer App for students on all plans. Students can access courses, community, and content through the app. However, a **branded mobile app** (with your own branding, not Kajabi's) is only available on the Pro plan at $399/mo annual.

**Teachable** offers iOS and Android student apps on all plans, including the $29/mo Starter tier. Students get push notifications, offline access, and a clean mobile learning experience. The apps carry Teachable branding unless you're on the Growth plan or higher.

**Winner: Teachable for accessibility** (mobile apps on all plans). **Kajabi for branding** (if you're on Pro). For most businesses, Teachable's mobile offering provides better value.

### 5. Payments & Revenue

**Kajabi** charges no platform transaction fees. Payment processing through Kajabi Payments runs 2.7–2.9% + $0.30 depending on your plan. The 3rd-party payment provider surcharge (0.5–2%) is a notable gotcha — if you want to use your own Stripe account, it costs extra.

**Teachable** charges a 7.5% transaction fee on the Starter plan — significant for any real revenue. Builder and above have 0% transaction fees. Teachable handles U.S. sales tax, global VAT, and GST automatically on all plans, which is a meaningful operational advantage.

Teachable also supports multiple payment methods through teachable:pay, including credit cards and PayPal, and manages global tax compliance.

**Winner: Depends on plan level.** Kajabi wins if you're comparing premium-to-premium (0% fees, no caveats). Teachable wins on tax handling and payment flexibility. Avoid Teachable's Starter plan if you're generating real revenue — the 7.5% fee adds up fast.

### 6. Community Features

**Kajabi** includes community spaces on all plans with access groups, discussion areas, and the ability to create engagement around courses. Community is integrated into the broader Kajabi ecosystem.

**Teachable** added community features that are available on all plans. Students can interact, discuss course material, and engage with each other. Lesson comments and community spaces create interaction points within the learning experience.

**Winner: Roughly even.** Both platforms offer functional community features. Neither is as robust as a dedicated community platform like Circle or Mighty Networks, but both are adequate for course-based communities.

### 7. Coaching & Services

**Kajabi** supports coaching programs as a product type, with scheduling, milestones, and client management. It's integrated into the broader product ecosystem, so coaches can bundle courses with coaching seamlessly.

**Teachable** supports coaching as a product type with 1:1 session booking, milestones, and structured coaching programs. The coaching features are functional and well-integrated with the course platform.

**Winner: Draw.** Both platforms offer solid coaching capabilities. Neither is a dedicated coaching platform, but both handle basic coaching workflows well.

### 8. Affiliate Programs

**Kajabi** includes an affiliate program — but only on the Growth plan ($199/mo annual) and above. Basic plan users cannot offer affiliate commissions, which locks out one of the most effective growth channels for course businesses.

**Teachable** includes affiliate program functionality on all plans, including the $29/mo Starter tier. You can set commission rates, track referrals, and manage affiliate payouts from day one.

**Winner: Teachable.** Affiliate access on all plans is a significant advantage for experts who want to leverage partners and students to drive enrollments.

### 9. Integrations & API

**Kajabi** supports third-party integrations via Zapier on all plans. However, webhooks require the Growth plan, and API access is locked to the Pro plan ($399/mo annual). This limits what you can build on lower tiers.

**Teachable** offers third-party integrations and Zapier on all plans. Webhooks scale from 1 (Starter) to unlimited (Advanced). API access is available starting at the Growth plan ($139/mo annual) with 10,000 requests/month, expanding on Advanced. OAuth support is available on Growth+.

**Winner: Teachable.** More accessible integrations at lower price points. API access at $139/mo vs. Kajabi's $399/mo is a significant difference for businesses that need custom integrations.

### 10. Analytics & Reporting

**Kajabi** provides integrated analytics that connect marketing activity (emails, funnels) to course enrollments and revenue. This end-to-end visibility is one of the genuine benefits of an all-in-one system — you can see which email sequence led to which purchase without stitching together data from multiple tools.

**Teachable** offers custom reporting on the Growth plan and above. Standard analytics cover revenue, enrollments, and student progress. The reporting is functional but doesn't provide the same marketing-to-enrollment pipeline view that Kajabi offers.

**Winner: Kajabi.** The integrated analytics across marketing and course delivery provide insights that are harder to replicate with separate tools.

## Pros & Cons Summary

### Kajabi

**Pros:**
- True all-in-one platform (email, funnels, pages, courses, community, coaching)
- No platform transaction fees on any plan
- Professional templates and polished design
- Integrated analytics across marketing and course delivery
- Strong automation capabilities (Growth plan+)
- Replaces 3–5 separate tools

**Cons:**
- Most expensive entry point in the market ($143/mo annual, $179/mo monthly)
- Aggressive feature-gating (affiliates, automations, cohorts locked to Growth+)
- 2,500 contact limit on Basic plan (contacts include all leads, not just students)
- 3rd-party Stripe surcharge of 0.5–2%
- API access requires Pro plan at $399/mo annual
- Course builder is functional but not best-in-class
- 2025 price increases raised costs 20–25% across all tiers

### Teachable

**Pros:**
- Much lower entry price ($29/mo annual)
- Stronger course builder with compliance, assessments, and auto-translations
- Affiliate program on all plans
- Mobile apps (iOS/Android) on all plans
- API access starting at $139/mo (vs. Kajabi's $399/mo)
- Automatic global tax handling (VAT, GST, U.S. sales tax)
- 7-day free trial + 30-day money-back guarantee

**Cons:**
- 7.5% transaction fee on Starter plan
- No built-in sales funnels or advanced landing pages
- Limited marketing automation (need external tools)
- Student limits are lower (100 on Starter, 1,000 on Builder)
- Requires external marketing stack for full capability
- Remove-branding requires Growth plan ($139/mo annual)
- Less polished website/page builder than Kajabi

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Kajabi If You Are:

**An expert building a marketing-driven business.** You sell courses, coaching, or memberships and your growth depends on email sequences, sales funnels, and landing pages. You want everything in one dashboard and you'd otherwise pay for ConvertKit ($29–$59/mo) + ClickFunnels ($97–$297/mo) + a course platform separately. Kajabi consolidates these costs — and at the Growth plan ($199/mo), it can actually be cheaper than running separate tools.

**An academy that values brand consistency.** You need a professional-looking website, course platform, and marketing system that all match. Kajabi's templates and integrated design system deliver a cohesive brand experience without hiring a designer.

**A company that prioritizes automation.** You want to automate the entire customer journey — from lead capture to email nurture to course enrollment to upsell — without connecting multiple platforms via Zapier.

### Choose Teachable If You Are:

**An expert who already has a marketing stack.** You use ConvertKit, Mailchimp, or ActiveCampaign for email. You have a WordPress site or Carrd landing page. You just need a solid place to host and sell courses. Teachable does this well at $69/mo (Builder, annual) without paying for marketing tools you won't use.

**An academy focused on course quality.** You prioritize the learning experience — compliance tracking, assessments, certificates, subtitles, and a clean student interface. Teachable's course builder is more refined than Kajabi's, and features like auto-translations help serve international audiences.

**A company adding customer education.** You need API access, webhooks, and integrations to connect training to your product. Teachable's Growth plan ($139/mo annual) gives you API access and 5 admin seats — Kajabi charges $399/mo for equivalent capabilities. Teachable's Enterprise tier adds SSO, dedicated support, and migration assistance for larger deployments.

**Anyone on a budget.** Teachable's Builder plan at $69/mo (annual) offers 10 products, 0% transaction fees, and real-time support. That's less than half the cost of Kajabi's cheapest plan.

## The Hidden Cost Factor: Your Full Stack

The biggest mistake in comparing Kajabi and Teachable is looking at platform price alone.

**Kajabi at $199/mo** replaces:
- Course platform (~$69–$139/mo)
- Email marketing (~$29–$79/mo)
- Funnel builder (~$97–$297/mo)
- Landing page tool (~$29–$99/mo)
- Website builder (~$12–$33/mo)

**Total replaced:** $236–$647/mo. At the Growth plan, Kajabi can genuinely save money for businesses that would otherwise pay for all these tools separately.

**Teachable at $69/mo** requires adding:
- Email marketing: $29–$79/mo
- Landing pages/funnels: $29–$297/mo (or none, if you don't need them)
- Website: $12–$33/mo (or use existing site)

**Total stack cost:** $69–$478/mo depending on your needs.

**The math:** If you need a full marketing stack, Kajabi's $199/mo (Growth, annual) is competitive. If you already have marketing tools or don't need funnels, Teachable at $69/mo saves real money.

## Verdict

**Kajabi** is the better choice for experts and academies that want a single platform to run their entire business — courses, marketing, website, email, and payments. The premium price is justified if you'd otherwise be paying for 3–5 separate tools. But the 2025 price increases, aggressive feature-gating, and $143/mo minimum make it a poor fit for anyone just starting out or primarily focused on course delivery.

**Teachable** is the better choice for most course-focused businesses. It offers a stronger course builder, lower prices, affiliate programs on all plans, and enough flexibility to grow. The trade-off is that you'll need external tools for advanced marketing — but if you already have those tools (or don't need them), Teachable delivers more value per dollar.

**For most experts launching their first course:** Start with Teachable's Builder plan ($69/mo annual). Build your audience, validate your offer, and invest in marketing tools as revenue justifies it. Switch to Kajabi later if you outgrow a modular stack and want consolidation.

**For established businesses doing $10,000+/mo:** Evaluate whether you're paying more in separate tool subscriptions than Kajabi's Growth plan ($199/mo annual) would cost. If yes, Kajabi's consolidation saves money and complexity. If you're happy with your stack, stay with Teachable.

**Neither platform is wrong.** The right choice depends on whether you value consolidation (Kajabi) or flexibility and lower cost (Teachable).
