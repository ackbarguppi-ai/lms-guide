# Teachable vs LearnDash: Hosted Simplicity or WordPress Power?

**Short answer:** Teachable is the better pick if you want a fully hosted platform that handles payments, taxes, and hosting so you can focus on teaching. LearnDash wins if you already run a WordPress site, need deep customization, or want to build a multi-instructor academy with granular control over every detail.

## What Each Platform Actually Is

Before diving into features, it helps to understand the fundamental difference — because it shapes everything else.

**Teachable** is a fully hosted platform. You sign up, build your course, connect a payment method, and start selling. Teachable handles hosting, security updates, SSL certificates, payment processing, and tax compliance. You don't touch a server. You don't install plugins. The trade-off is that you're working within Teachable's walls — their templates, their feature set, their rules.

**LearnDash** is a WordPress plugin. You install it on a WordPress site that you own and host. This means you control everything: the design, the server, the database, every plugin in the stack. The trade-off is that "everything" includes responsibility for hosting, backups, security, updates, and making sure your plugins play nicely together.

LearnDash also offers **cloud-hosted subscription plans** (starting at $29/mo) that bundle hosting, domains, and the LMS plugin — essentially giving you a more Teachable-like managed experience. But even the cloud version runs on WordPress under the hood, so you're still in WordPress territory.

This distinction — hosted SaaS vs. WordPress plugin — is the single biggest factor in choosing between them. Everything else flows from it.

## Course Building & Content

### Teachable

Teachable's course builder is straightforward. You create sections, add lessons (video, text, PDF, audio), and arrange them with drag-and-drop. The AI-assisted course creation tools can generate outlines and content scaffolding — useful for getting started quickly.

Content types include video lessons, text, PDFs, quizzes, and downloadable files. You can drip content on a schedule, require lesson completion before advancing, and issue certificates. It covers the essentials well but doesn't go deep on interactive content.

**Quizzes** support auto-graded multiple choice and short-answer assessments. Functional, but limited compared to LearnDash's quiz engine.

### LearnDash

LearnDash's course builder is also drag-and-drop, organized in a hierarchy: courses → lessons → topics → quizzes. This deeper structure suits complex, multi-module curricula — think professional certifications or academic programs.

The **quiz engine** is where LearnDash genuinely outclasses Teachable. It supports eight question types (single choice, multiple choice, free text, sorting, matching, essay, fill-in-the-blank, and survey), plus advanced features like question banks, randomized question pools, time limits, retry restrictions, and custom result messages based on score ranges. The AI quiz builder can generate questions from your content.

LearnDash also supports **assignments** with manual or auto-approval, **focus mode** (a distraction-free learning view), and **video progression** (requiring students to watch a video before proceeding). These features make it significantly stronger for structured education.

**Winner: LearnDash** — especially for academies and companies that need robust assessments and structured learning paths.

## Sales & Marketing

### Teachable

Teachable treats selling as a first-class feature. Built into every plan:

- **teachable:pay** — integrated payment processing with support for credit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Pay
- **Automated tax handling** — US sales tax, international VAT, and GST calculated and remitted automatically
- **Coupons, bundles, and upsells** — including order bumps at checkout
- **Abandoned cart recovery emails** — automated follow-ups to recover lost sales
- **Built-in affiliate program** — recruit partners to promote your courses
- **Built-in email marketing** — basic email tools for student communication
- **Student referrals** — incentivize word-of-mouth

For an expert who wants to sell a course without assembling a marketing stack, Teachable offers a remarkably complete out-of-the-box solution.

### LearnDash

LearnDash handles payments through Stripe, PayPal, or Razorpay integration. You can sell one-time purchases, subscriptions, memberships, and bundles. Coupon functionality is built in.

Beyond that, you're assembling your own stack:

- **Email marketing:** WooCommerce + Mailchimp, FluentCRM, or ActiveCampaign
- **Affiliates:** AffiliateWP or similar plugin
- **Cart/checkout:** WooCommerce (free, but adds complexity)
- **Tax handling:** TaxJar, Quaderno, or manual compliance
- **Abandoned cart:** WooCommerce extension or third-party tool

This isn't necessarily a weakness — it means you can choose best-in-class tools for each job. But it requires research, setup time, and ongoing maintenance.

**Winner: Teachable** — for built-in sales and marketing. LearnDash can match or exceed Teachable's capabilities, but you're building it yourself.

## Design & Customization

### Teachable

Teachable provides templates and a visual editor for your school's website. You can customize colors, fonts, logos, and layout within the platform's framework. CSS snippets are available for deeper tweaks. On the Growth plan, you can remove Teachable branding entirely.

It looks professional out of the box, but you're constrained to Teachable's design system. If you want something truly unique, you'll hit the walls quickly.

### LearnDash

Because LearnDash runs on WordPress, you have essentially unlimited design control. Pair it with a theme like BuddyBoss (popular in the LearnDash ecosystem) or Astra, and you can build any design you want. Custom post types, page builders (Elementor, Beaver Builder, Divi), custom CSS, custom PHP — the sky is the limit.

This is the core WordPress advantage: your LMS is just one part of a full website that you control completely.

**Winner: LearnDash** — by a wide margin, if design control matters to you.

## Community & Engagement

### Teachable

Teachable includes a built-in community feature — a space for students to interact, discuss course content, and connect with instructors. It also supports lesson comments for discussion within courses. Basic but functional, and it's included without extra setup.

### LearnDash

LearnDash doesn't include a native community, but the WordPress ecosystem fills this gap well. BuddyBoss is the most popular pairing — it adds social networking features, groups, activity feeds, messaging, and forums tightly integrated with LearnDash courses. bbPress is another option for simpler forum-style discussions.

The result can be far more powerful than Teachable's built-in community, but it's another plugin (and cost) to manage.

**Winner: Tie** — Teachable is simpler; LearnDash + BuddyBoss is more powerful. Depends on your needs.

## Scalability & Multi-Instructor Support

### Teachable

Teachable supports multiple admin users (1 on Starter/Builder, 5 on Growth/Advanced) and custom user roles. But it's fundamentally designed for a single expert or small team running one school. There's no native multi-instructor marketplace model.

### LearnDash

LearnDash's **Instructor Role add-on** ($99/yr) enables front-end course creation by multiple instructors — useful for building a course marketplace or academy with multiple subject-matter experts. The **Groups Management add-on** ($99/yr) lets you create organizations, assign team leaders, and manage cohort-based learning.

For companies running employee training, LearnDash's group management, reporting (via ProPanel), and WordPress user management offer significantly more flexibility than Teachable.

**Winner: LearnDash** — clearly built for multi-instructor academies and corporate training.

## Mobile Experience

### Teachable

Every Teachable plan includes access to iOS and Android student apps. Students download the Teachable app, log in, and access their courses. It's not a custom-branded app (it says "Teachable"), but it works out of the box with no extra cost.

### LearnDash

LearnDash offers a mobile app add-on at **$1,199/yr**, which includes done-for-you setup, custom branding, app store submission, and ongoing updates. The app supports offline learning, cross-device progress syncing, certificates, and drip content.

It's a premium product with a premium price. Most LearnDash users rely on responsive web design instead.

**Winner: Teachable** — mobile apps at no extra cost vs. $1,199/yr is a clear gap for most users.

## Ease of Use

### Teachable

Sign up, follow the onboarding flow, build your course, connect payments, publish. An expert with no technical background can launch a course in a weekend. Teachable handles the infrastructure, so you never think about hosting, SSL certificates, or plugin conflicts.

The trade-off: when something isn't available in Teachable, your options are limited. You can't install a plugin to fill a gap. You're dependent on Teachable's roadmap.

### LearnDash

Even with the improved onboarding wizard and cloud plans, LearnDash assumes WordPress familiarity. Self-hosted setups require choosing hosting, installing WordPress, configuring the plugin, selecting a compatible theme, and potentially adding WooCommerce for advanced selling.

The learning curve is real. But once you're past setup, the WordPress admin is a powerful and flexible environment — especially if you already know it.

**Winner: Teachable** — significantly easier for non-technical users.

## Integrations & Ecosystem

### Teachable

Teachable offers native integrations with popular tools, Zapier connectivity (1 webhook on Starter, up to unlimited on Advanced), and a public API for custom development. The API has rate limits (100/minute) and volume limits (10,000–25,000 requests/month depending on plan).

### LearnDash

Being a WordPress plugin, LearnDash can integrate with the entire WordPress ecosystem — thousands of plugins for email, CRM, analytics, membership, e-commerce, and more. Direct integrations exist for Zapier, WooCommerce, bbPress, BuddyBoss, and many others.

The plugin ecosystem is both LearnDash's greatest strength and its biggest maintenance burden. More integrations mean more potential conflicts, more updates to manage, and more things that can break.

**Winner: LearnDash** — more integration options, though with more maintenance responsibility.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Teachable If You're…

**A solo expert launching your first course.** You want to teach, not manage infrastructure. Teachable lets you go from idea to selling in days, with payments, taxes, and hosting handled. The Starter plan at $29/mo (annual) is a low-risk entry point — upgrade to Builder at $69/mo when the 7.5% transaction fee starts eating into revenue.

**An expert who values simplicity over control.** You don't want to think about hosting, security updates, plugin compatibility, or tax compliance. You want a platform that works, looks professional, and lets you focus on content and students.

**Someone who needs mobile apps without a big investment.** Teachable's included iOS/Android apps give your students a native mobile experience at no extra cost.

### Choose LearnDash If You're…

**Building a multi-instructor academy.** You need multiple experts creating and managing their own courses, with group management, organizational structures, and detailed reporting. LearnDash's add-ons are purpose-built for this.

**A company setting up employee or customer training.** You need group enrollment, team leaders, progress tracking across departments, and integration with existing WordPress infrastructure. LearnDash's Groups Management and ProPanel deliver here.

**A WordPress-savvy expert who wants full control.** You already run a WordPress site, you're comfortable with plugins and hosting, and you want your LMS deeply integrated with your existing web presence. The $199/yr plugin license is cost-effective if you already have hosting sorted.

**Someone who needs advanced assessments.** If your courses require complex quizzes with multiple question types, question banks, time limits, and detailed result analysis, LearnDash's quiz engine is in a different league.

### The Budget Question

Here's the nuance that gets lost in most comparisons: **LearnDash looks cheaper on paper but can cost more in practice.**

The $199/yr plugin license is attractive until you add hosting, a theme, add-ons, tax compliance, and the hours spent on setup and maintenance. Teachable's $69/mo Builder plan ($828/yr) includes everything — and your time has value too.

Conversely, if you're already paying for WordPress hosting and you're comfortable in that ecosystem, adding LearnDash at $199/yr is genuinely affordable. Context matters.

## The Verdict

**Teachable** is the right choice for most independent experts. It removes the infrastructure burden, includes sales tools that actually work, handles tax compliance automatically, and lets you launch fast. The pricing is transparent — what you see is what you pay.

**LearnDash** is the right choice for academies, multi-instructor setups, and companies that need the flexibility and control of WordPress. It's also the better fit for technically comfortable experts who want to own every piece of their stack.

Neither platform is objectively "better." They solve the same problem — selling online education — with fundamentally different philosophies. Teachable says "we'll handle it." LearnDash says "you're in control."

Pick the philosophy that matches how you want to run your business.
