# Thinkific vs Kajabi: Which Platform Actually Fits Your Business?

Thinkific and Kajabi both help you sell online courses, but they solve different problems. **Thinkific is a course-first platform** — strong on learning tools, flexible pricing, and unlimited courses on every plan. **Kajabi is an all-in-one business platform** — it bundles email marketing, sales funnels, landing pages, and course delivery into a single dashboard.

The right choice depends on what you already have and what you need most. If you have an email tool and just need a place to build and sell courses, Thinkific saves you money. If you want one platform to handle everything from lead capture to course delivery, Kajabi consolidates the stack — at a premium.

Here's how they compare across every category that matters.

## Course Building and Delivery

Both platforms offer drag-and-drop course builders with support for video, text, audio, PDFs, and embeds. Both support drip scheduling, content locking, and completion certificates.

### Where Thinkific leads:
- **Unlimited courses on every plan** — Kajabi limits you to 5 products on Basic
- **Stronger assessment tools** — quizzes, exams, surveys, assignments, plus an integration with Brillium for professional-grade assessments
- **AI quiz generator** — creates questions from your course content automatically
- **SCORM compliance** on the Plus plan — important for companies delivering compliance training
- **Randomized question banks** — serve unique quiz sets to each student (Grow plan and above)

### Where Kajabi leads:
- **Built-in live video** — host sessions with up to 200 participants without Zoom or third-party tools
- **Native coaching tools** — built-in scheduler, session notes, one-on-one and group programs
- **Automatic session recording** — live sessions are recorded and added as lessons
- **Cohort-based courses** — time-bound, group programs (Growth plan and above)
- **AI content repurposing (Creator Studio)** — turns course videos into social posts, emails, and blog content

### Verdict: Course Building
Thinkific wins on **depth of learning tools** — more assessment types, SCORM support, and unlimited courses. Kajabi wins on **live engagement** — native video, coaching, and cohort programs without third-party tools.

## Marketing and Sales Tools

This is where the platforms diverge most.

### Thinkific's marketing stack:
- Website builder with themes (basic customization — no animations, limited mobile control)
- Landing pages (functional but limited — can't reorder elements or change color schemes)
- 2 funnel types: free lesson and sales page (minimal customization)
- 2 email automation types: sales flow and abandoned cart
- No email segmentation or tagging
- Order bumps, coupons, bundles, upsells, and gifting
- Abandoned cart recovery emails
- Affiliate reporting (Grow plan)

### Kajabi's marketing stack:
- Website builder with advanced drag-and-drop (animations, desktop/mobile/tablet views)
- Unlimited landing pages with modern templates and full customization
- Sales funnel templates for product launches, webinars, coaching, free books, and more
- Full email marketing suite: broadcasts, automated sequences, tagging, segmentation
- Blog with SEO settings and email opt-in forms
- Affiliate programs (Growth plan and above)
- Advanced automations: tag-based triggers, conditional flows, multi-step sequences

### Verdict: Marketing
**Kajabi wins decisively.** If marketing is central to your business — email sequences, funnels, landing pages — Kajabi provides a complete toolkit. Thinkific's marketing features are functional but basic; most serious businesses will need to add a third-party email platform (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign) and potentially a funnel builder.

## Community Features

Both platforms offer community spaces, but the depth differs significantly.

### Thinkific communities:
- 1 community on Basic/Start, 3 on Grow, unlimited on Plus
- Organized by "spaces" (similar to Slack channels)
- Text, image, and video posts with threaded replies and @mentions
- Scheduled live events (via third-party video tools)
- Can bundle community access with courses for membership packages
- Direct messaging

### Kajabi communities:
- Access groups for organizing members and discussions
- Text, audio, video, image, and PDF posts
- Direct messaging between members
- Built-in live streaming for community events (no third-party tools)
- Challenges with templates and rewards
- Gamification: points, badges, leaderboards
- Offline and online meetup organization

### Verdict: Community
**Kajabi wins.** The gamification, challenges, and built-in live streaming make Kajabi communities significantly more engaging. Thinkific communities are functional but lack the engagement tools that keep members active long-term.

## Website and Design

**Kajabi** offers a more polished page builder with modern templates, animation controls, and responsive design editing for desktop, mobile, and tablet. Every element is deeply customizable. It also includes a built-in blog.

**Thinkific** provides a clean site builder with themes, but customization is more limited. You can't reorder sections within templates, change color schemes, or add animations through the visual editor. Advanced customization requires HTML/CSS editing (Start plan and above).

### Verdict: Design
**Kajabi wins** for non-technical users who want professional-looking pages out of the box. **Thinkific wins** for technical users who are comfortable with code and want full control via HTML/CSS.

## Integrations and Extensibility

**Thinkific** has a dedicated app store with a wider range of native integrations, plus Zapier support. Its API is available on the Grow plan ($199/mo). Webhooks are available on Grow and above. Since Thinkific is more focused on course delivery, the integrations fill in the marketing, scheduling, and communication gaps.

**Kajabi** has a solid set of native integrations and Zapier support. However, API access is locked behind the Pro plan ($499/mo), and webhooks require the Growth plan ($249/mo). Because Kajabi builds more features natively, fewer integrations are needed — but you pay more when you need to connect external tools.

### Verdict: Integrations
**Thinkific wins** on openness and flexibility — more integrations, lower-tier API access, and no penalties for using your own payment processor.

## Who Should Choose Thinkific

### Experts selling courses and coaching
You have existing marketing tools (email, social, maybe a website) and need a dedicated platform to build and deliver courses. Thinkific's $49/mo entry point and unlimited courses make it the most cost-effective starting point. Add your own email tool and you're running.

**Best plan:** Start ($99/mo) for memberships, certificates, and unlimited live sessions via Zoom.

### Academies running training businesses
You need robust assessment tools, multiple courses, and the ability to scale without hitting product caps. Thinkific's unlimited courses, exam integrations, and SCORM support (on Plus) make it strong for professional training at scale.

**Best plan:** Grow ($199/mo) for API access, analytics, group orders, and priority support. Plus for enterprise needs.

### Companies adding customer education
You need SSO, SCORM compliance, learning paths, and CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot). Thinkific's Plus plan is purpose-built for this.

**Best plan:** Plus (custom pricing).

---

## Who Should Choose Kajabi

### Experts building a full business around their knowledge
You don't just want to host courses — you want to run your entire business from one place. Email marketing, funnels, landing pages, coaching, community, and courses all in one dashboard. You're willing to pay a premium to avoid managing multiple tools.

**Best plan:** Growth ($199/mo annual) for affiliates, advanced automations, and 50 products.

### Experts who prioritize live engagement
You run coaching programs, live workshops, or cohort-based courses and want built-in video sessions, scheduling, and community challenges without piecing together third-party tools.

**Best plan:** Growth ($199/mo annual) for cohort courses and advanced community tools.

### Marketing-driven businesses
Your revenue depends on email sequences, automated funnels, and conversion-optimized landing pages. Kajabi's marketing stack is meaningfully better than Thinkific's, and consolidating tools saves operational complexity.

**Best plan:** Growth ($199/mo annual) or Pro ($399/mo annual) depending on contact volume.

## Who Should NOT Choose Each Platform

### Don't choose Thinkific if:
- You need sophisticated email marketing and don't want to manage a separate tool
- Live video and native coaching are critical to your programs
- You want beautiful landing pages without touching code

### Don't choose Kajabi if:
- You're price-sensitive and already have marketing tools — you'll pay $130+/mo more for features you may not use
- You need more than 5 products on the entry plan — Kajabi's Basic is restrictive
- SCORM compliance or advanced assessments matter — Kajabi doesn't offer them
- You want to use your own Stripe account without paying a surcharge
- You need API access without paying $499/mo

---

## Final Verdict

**Choose Thinkific** if course quality, affordability, and flexibility are your priorities. It's the better learning platform — more assessment types, unlimited courses, SCORM support, and a lower entry price. You'll need to bring your own marketing tools, but many businesses already have those.

**Choose Kajabi** if you want a single platform for your entire business and are willing to pay for it. The marketing and sales tools are genuinely strong, and the all-in-one convenience is real — if you'd otherwise spend $100–200/mo on separate email, funnel, and landing page tools, Kajabi's price premium makes sense.

**For most experts just starting out**, Thinkific is the pragmatic choice. Start at $49/mo, sell your first courses, and add tools as revenue grows.

**For established experts doing $5,000+/mo** who want to consolidate their stack, Kajabi's Growth plan ($199/mo annual) is a strong all-in-one option — if you'll use the email marketing and funnel tools.

Neither platform is a wrong choice. They're both mature, reliable, and well-supported. The question isn't which is "better" — it's which matches the business you're actually building.
