# Teachable vs Podia: Which Platform Should You Choose in 2026?

**Short answer:** Teachable is the stronger choice if you need a dedicated course platform with robust student analytics, built-in certificates, and SEO-optimized pages. Podia is better if you want an affordable all-in-one storefront for selling courses alongside digital downloads, webinars, and community — without paying for features you don't need.

Neither platform is universally "better." The right pick depends on whether you're an independent expert launching a first course, an academy scaling multiple programs, or a company building internal training.

## Pricing Breakdown

### Teachable Pricing (2026)

Teachable offers five tiers. The key threshold: transaction fees disappear at the Builder plan ($89/mo).

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Transaction Fee | Published Products | Admin Users |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | $0 | $1 + 10% | 1 | 1 |
| **Starter** | $39 | $29 | 7.5% | 1 | 1 |
| **Builder** | $89 | $69 | 0% | 5 | 5 |
| **Growth** | $189 | $139 | 0% | 25 | 20 |
| **Advanced** | $399 | $309 | 0% | 100 | 100 |

All plans include integrated payment processing through Stripe Express with support for 200+ countries. Standard payment processor fees (typically 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction via Stripe) apply on every plan — these are not Teachable fees.

**Key unlock points:**
- **Builder ($89/mo):** 0% transaction fees, affiliate program, certificates, live chat support, custom domain
- **Growth ($189/mo):** White-label site, custom admin permissions, subtitle/translation generation
- **Advanced ($399/mo):** 100 published products, unlimited integrations

### Podia Pricing (2026)

Podia uses four tiers with email marketing as a separate add-on. Transaction fees drop to 0% only on the top-tier Shaker plan.

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Transaction Fee | Key Limits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | $0 | 10% | 1 download, 1 coaching product; courses are draft-only |
| **Starter** | $9 | $4 | 8% | Custom domain, 1 download, 1 coaching product |
| **Mover** | $39 | $33 | 5% | Unlimited products, webinars, bundles, drip courses |
| **Shaker** | $89 | $59 | 0% | Everything in Mover + affiliate marketing |

**Email marketing add-on pricing** (separate from plan cost):
- Under 100 subscribers: Free
- 100–500 subscribers: $9/mo
- Scales up to $330/mo for 100,000+ subscribers

**Key unlock points:**
- **Mover ($39/mo):** Where Podia becomes genuinely useful — unlimited products, custom domain, remove branding, drip courses, payment plans
- **Shaker ($89/mo):** 0% transaction fees, affiliate marketing

### Price-to-Value Comparison

At the **$39/mo tier**, Podia's Mover plan delivers significantly more than Teachable's Starter plan: unlimited products vs. one published product, 5% vs. 7.5% transaction fees, and webinar support included.

At the **$89/mo tier**, both platforms eliminate transaction fees and add affiliate marketing. Teachable adds certificates, advanced analytics, and live chat. Podia keeps things simpler with unlimited everything and lower annual pricing ($59/mo vs. $69/mo).

At **$139+/mo**, Teachable pulls ahead for organizations needing team management, white-label branding, and enterprise controls. Podia has no equivalent tier.

## Category-by-Category Breakdown

### Course Creation and Delivery

**Teachable** was built as a course platform first, and it shows. The course builder supports multimedia lessons with text, video, images, and screen recordings. Pages are SEO-optimized out of the box. The student experience includes auto-play controls, playback speed adjustment, and a progress tracker. Graded quizzes can enforce minimum pass marks and limit retake attempts — useful for compliance training and certification programs.

Built-in course completion certificates with customizable templates are a significant differentiator. No third-party workarounds needed.

**Podia** offers a clean, functional course builder with drip content, quizzes, and a straightforward course player with table-of-contents navigation. It handles the fundamentals well. However, quizzes are less configurable (no graded pass/fail gating), and certificates require third-party tools like Zapier.

**Edge:** Teachable, especially for structured learning programs, academies, and any use case requiring assessments or certificates.

### Website and Storefront

**Teachable** provides the CoursePro page editor with 120+ design blocks, drag-and-drop layout, an image library, and template options for high-converting sales pages. You can add transitions, shadows, and visual effects. It's designed to sell courses specifically.

**Podia** takes a broader storefront approach. The website builder creates homepages, landing pages, review pages, contact pages, and sales funnels. It's simpler than Teachable's editor but more versatile — you're building a full website, not just course pages. Blog functionality is included on all plans.

**Edge:** Podia for a complete web presence; Teachable for polished course-specific sales pages.

### Digital Products Beyond Courses

**Teachable** is primarily a course and coaching platform. Selling standalone digital downloads, templates, or ebooks isn't its strength.

**Podia** was designed as a multi-product storefront from the start. You can sell courses, digital downloads (ebooks, templates, audio files, video files), coaching sessions, webinars, and product bundles — all from one store. This flexibility is a core advantage.

**Edge:** Podia, decisively. If you sell anything beyond courses, Podia handles it natively.

### Community Features

**Teachable** offers community features within its platform, allowing students to interact with each other and with instructors.

**Podia** provides a dedicated community product with topic-based organization, discussion forums, and the ability to bundle community access with courses or sell it standalone.

**Edge:** Roughly even. Both provide community tools, though Podia's standalone community product offers more flexibility in how you package and sell access.

### Webinars and Live Content

**Teachable** does not offer native webinar functionality. You'll need to integrate with third-party webinar tools.

**Podia** integrates natively with Zoom and YouTube Live, letting you sell access to live webinars, workshops, and classes. You can also sell replay access after the event. This is available on the Mover plan ($39/mo).

**Edge:** Podia, clearly. Native webinar selling is a significant advantage for experts who teach live.

### Email Marketing

**Teachable** relies on integrations with third-party email providers like MailChimp and ConvertKit. You'll need external tools for any serious email marketing.

**Podia** offers email marketing as a built-in add-on with broadcast editing, subscriber segmentation by product and revenue, and scalable pricing. It's not as powerful as dedicated email platforms, but it keeps everything in one place.

**Edge:** Podia. Having email built into the platform (even as an add-on) reduces tool sprawl and simplifies workflows.

### Analytics and Reporting

**Teachable** provides robust built-in analytics: course completion rates, video engagement and play rates, quiz score statistics, and a student leaderboard ranked by progress and enrollment date. These reports are available within the platform without any third-party setup.

**Podia** depends heavily on third-party analytics. Google Analytics and Facebook Pixel are the recommended tracking tools. You won't get course-specific engagement data from Podia itself.

**Edge:** Teachable, strongly. Built-in learning analytics are essential for understanding student outcomes, improving course content, and demonstrating value — especially for academies and companies.

### Payment Processing and Checkout

**Teachable** supports Stripe Express, PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. The checkout experience is streamlined into a single page — no account creation required for students. Teachable handles global payments and tax solutions across 200+ countries and regions.

**Podia** integrates with Stripe and PayPal. The checkout process is functional with embeddable buttons and links. Payment plans are available on the Mover plan and above.

**Edge:** Teachable. More payment methods, single-page checkout, and global tax handling give it a smoother buying experience.

### Membership Sites

**Teachable** supports membership sites with tiered access levels, allowing you to gate exclusive content behind different subscription tiers.

**Podia** offers unlimited members and unlimited membership plans with no caps on enrollment. This is a meaningful difference for experts building large membership communities.

**Edge:** Podia for flexibility and no artificial limits; Teachable for structured tiered access.

### Integrations

**Podia** connects with a broader range of third-party apps: ConvertKit, HotJar, Pinterest, and Zapier (which opens access to 1,000+ additional tools).

**Teachable** offers fewer native integrations but covers the essentials: MailChimp, Google Analytics, Vizia (for polls and CTAs), and Zapier.

**Edge:** Podia offers more native integrations, though Zapier availability on both platforms largely levels the field.

### Mobile Experience

**Teachable** includes a native iOS student app on all plans, giving learners a dedicated mobile experience for consuming course content.

**Podia** does not offer a native mobile app. Students access content through mobile-responsive web pages.

**Edge:** Teachable. A native app improves student engagement and course completion rates.

### Customer Support

**Teachable** provides a multi-channel support system: knowledge base, webinar workshops, community forums, TeachableU training programs, a help center with ticket generation, and live chat (8 AM–8 PM, 7 days/week). Live chat support starts at the Builder plan ($89/mo).

**Podia** offers 7-days-a-week support on all plans, including the free tier. Support includes live chat during business hours, email, and a help center with 150+ articles. The team is known for being responsive and personal.

**Edge:** Teachable has more support channels; Podia has support available on every plan including free. Depends on what you value more.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Teachable If You Are...

**An expert building a structured course business.** If courses are your primary product and you want robust course-specific tools — graded quizzes, completion certificates, SEO-optimized pages, built-in analytics, and a native mobile app — Teachable provides a more complete learning experience. The Builder plan at $89/mo ($69/mo annual) is the sweet spot where transaction fees disappear and the full course toolkit unlocks.

**An academy scaling multiple programs.** The Growth plan ($189/mo) and Advanced plan ($399/mo) offer team management with custom admin permissions, white-label branding, and support for 25–100 published products. If you're running an online school with multiple instructors and need organizational controls, Teachable is built for this.

**A company delivering training or compliance content.** Graded assessments with pass/fail gating, completion certificates, detailed student analytics, and multi-admin support make Teachable suitable for corporate training and professional development programs.

### Choose Podia If You Are...

**An expert selling multiple product types.** If you sell courses alongside ebooks, templates, coaching sessions, webinars, and community access, Podia's multi-product storefront eliminates the need to stitch together multiple tools. One platform, one store, one checkout.

**A solo expert who wants simplicity and low cost.** Podia's Mover plan ($39/mo) gives you unlimited products, webinars, custom domain, drip courses, and payment plans. At the same price, Teachable limits you to one published product with a 7.5% transaction fee. For budget-conscious experts, this math is hard to argue with.

**An expert who teaches live.** Native webinar selling (via Zoom/YouTube Live integration) with replay access is something Teachable simply doesn't offer without third-party tools. If live workshops, classes, or Q&A sessions are part of your business model, Podia makes this easy.

## The Verdict

**Teachable is a course platform. Podia is a product platform.** That distinction drives every meaningful difference between them.

If you're building a business where courses are the product — where student progress, assessment, certification, and learning outcomes matter — Teachable provides deeper tools for the job. Its analytics, quiz engine, certificate builder, and mobile app create a more complete learning environment. Academies and companies with compliance or credentialing needs should lean Teachable.

If you're building a business where courses are one of several products — where you also sell downloads, run paid webinars, offer coaching, and build community — Podia's all-in-one approach is more practical and more affordable. You avoid stitching together multiple tools, and the Mover plan at $39/mo delivers genuine value that Teachable can't match at the same price.

For most independent experts just getting started, **Podia's Mover plan ($39/mo)** offers the best starting point: unlimited products, functional course tools, webinar support, and a clean storefront. As your business grows and you need deeper course infrastructure, Teachable's Builder plan ($89/mo) becomes the natural upgrade.

For academies and companies, **Teachable's Growth or Advanced plans** are where the platform earns its premium — with team management, white-label branding, and the analytics depth that organizational buyers expect.

Neither platform is wrong. The question is whether your business revolves around courses specifically, or courses as part of a broader product mix.
