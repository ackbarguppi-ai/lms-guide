## Priority Tiers

- **Tier 1 (1–15):** Highest search volume, most direct Thinkific competitors, write first.
- **Tier 2 (16–28):** Strong enterprise/education players, high brand recognition.
- **Tier 3 (29–40):** Niche or emerging platforms, valuable for long-tail SEO.

## Tier 1 — Write First (Highest Priority)

### 1. Kajabi
- **Description:** All-in-one platform for online courses, coaching, memberships, podcasts, and marketing funnels. Known for built-in email marketing and sales pipeline tools.
- **Target Audience:** Experts, coaches, solopreneurs
- **Popularity:** Top 3 course platform by search volume; ~50,000+ active creators
- **Key Differentiators:** All-in-one (no third-party tools needed), built-in email/funnels, premium pricing positions it as "the business platform"
- **Why Priority:** #1 Thinkific competitor by search volume. "Thinkific vs Kajabi" is the most-searched comparison.

### 2. Teachable
- **Description:** Online course and coaching platform focused on simplicity. Owned by Hotmart since 2020. Strong brand in the creator economy.
- **Target Audience:** Experts, individual course creators
- **Popularity:** Top 3 course platform; one of the earliest major players
- **Key Differentiators:** Easy setup, integrated payments, coaching products, strong brand recognition
- **Why Priority:** Perennial #1 or #2 Thinkific alternative in every comparison article.

### 3. Udemy / Udemy Business
- **Description:** World's largest open course marketplace (consumer). Udemy Business is the B2B corporate training arm with curated content library.
- **Target Audience:** Consumer learners (marketplace); Enterprise employee training (Udemy Business)
- **Popularity:** 75M+ learners, 220K+ courses. Udemy Business serves 16,000+ organizations.
- **Key Differentiators:** Massive marketplace reach; Udemy Business offers pre-built content library vs. create-your-own
- **Why Priority:** Huge search volume. Very different model but constantly compared.

### 4. Skillshare
- **Description:** Subscription-based creative learning community. Focuses on creative skills (design, illustration, photography, writing).
- **Target Audience:** Creative professionals, hobbyists
- **Popularity:** 12M+ registered users, 30K+ classes
- **Key Differentiators:** Subscription model (Netflix for learning), community-driven, royalty-based creator payments
- **Why Priority:** High brand awareness, frequently searched against course platforms.

### 5. Podia
- **Description:** Simple platform for selling courses, digital downloads, webinars, and memberships. Known for clean UX and no transaction fees.
- **Target Audience:** Experts, solopreneurs, small creators
- **Popularity:** Growing mid-market player; strong in creator community
- **Key Differentiators:** Zero transaction fees on all plans, built-in email, simple pricing, clean interface
- **Why Priority:** Direct Thinkific competitor, often in "top 5 course platforms" lists.

### 6. LearnWorlds
- **Description:** Feature-rich course platform with advanced interactive video, built-in community, and white-label capabilities.
- **Target Audience:** Experts, training companies, SMBs
- **Popularity:** Fast-growing; strong in European and education markets
- **Key Differentiators:** Interactive video (in-video questions/branching), SCORM support, white-label, built-in website builder
- **Why Priority:** Increasingly mentioned as Thinkific alternative, especially for advanced features.

### 7. Docebo
- **Description:** AI-powered enterprise learning platform. Strong in corporate training, compliance, and extended enterprise learning.
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise, mid-market corporate
- **Popularity:** Publicly traded (DCBO), 3,800+ customers, Gartner Magic Quadrant leader
- **Key Differentiators:** AI-powered content recommendations, social learning, multi-audience (employees, partners, customers), strong integrations
- **Why Priority:** Top enterprise LMS, shows up in many comparison searches.

### 8. TalentLMS
- **Description:** Cloud-based LMS focused on ease of use for SMBs and mid-market. Part of Epignosis group.
- **Target Audience:** SMB, mid-market corporate training
- **Popularity:** 70,000+ organizations use it; consistently top-rated on G2/Capterra
- **Key Differentiators:** Free tier available, extremely fast setup, gamification, affordable pricing, strong SMB fit
- **Why Priority:** Bridge between course platforms and enterprise LMS; frequently compared to both categories.

### 9. Moodle
- **Description:** World's most-used open-source LMS. Powers universities, schools, and corporate training worldwide.
- **Target Audience:** Education (K-12, higher ed), corporate training (via Moodle Workplace)
- **Popularity:** 400M+ users, 340K+ sites in 240+ countries. ~20% global LMS market share.
- **Key Differentiators:** Free/open-source, massive plugin ecosystem, extreme customizability, self-hosted or cloud (MoodleCloud)
- **Why Priority:** Largest LMS by installations globally. Major search volume.

### 10. Canvas (by Instructure)
- **Description:** Modern cloud-based LMS dominating US higher education. Known for clean UX and robust API.
- **Target Audience:** Higher education, K-12
- **Popularity:** #1 LMS in US higher education (~33% market share); used by 6,000+ institutions
- **Key Differentiators:** Modern UX, open API, SpeedGrader, strong mobile app, Canvas Network for open courses
- **Why Priority:** Dominant in education market; high search volume.

### 11. Blackboard / Anthology
- **Description:** Legacy LMS giant, now part of Anthology (merged 2022). Powers thousands of universities globally.
- **Target Audience:** Higher education, K-12
- **Popularity:** ~25% of US higher ed market (declining); major global presence
- **Key Differentiators:** Deep institutional integrations, Ally accessibility tool, comprehensive analytics, established vendor relationships
- **Why Priority:** Still massive installed base despite declining share. Well-known brand.

### 12. Cornerstone OnDemand
- **Description:** Enterprise talent management and learning platform. Acquired Saba (2020) and EdCast (2022). Now offers "Cornerstone Galaxy" unified platform.
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise, large corporate
- **Popularity:** 75M+ users, 7,000+ customers. Gartner/Forrester leader. Taken private by Clearlake Capital (2022) at $5.2B.
- **Key Differentiators:** Full talent suite (learning + performance + recruiting + content), AI-powered skills intelligence, compliance training, global scale
- **Why Priority:** Explicitly requested. Top enterprise LMS globally.

### 13. Saba (now Cornerstone)
- **Description:** Enterprise LMS and talent management platform acquired by Cornerstone in 2020. Being migrated to Cornerstone Galaxy platform.
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise, large corporate
- **Popularity:** Was a top 5 enterprise LMS pre-acquisition; legacy customers still on Saba Cloud
- **Key Differentiators:** Strong in compliance and regulated industries, social/collaborative learning features, now integrated into Cornerstone Galaxy
- **Why Priority:** Explicitly requested. Important for SEO — many orgs still searching "Saba LMS" during migration.

### 14. SAP Litmos
- **Description:** Cloud-based corporate LMS with built-in content library. Acquired by Francisco Partners from SAP in 2022, rebranded to just "Litmos."
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise, mid-market corporate
- **Popularity:** 30M+ users across 150 countries
- **Key Differentiators:** Built-in content library (2,000+ courses), rapid deployment, strong compliance training, video assessments
- **Why Priority:** Well-known enterprise LMS brand; "SAP Litmos" still heavily searched.

### 15. Absorb LMS
- **Description:** Cloud-based corporate LMS focused on learner engagement and admin efficiency. Strong in mid-market and enterprise.
- **Target Audience:** Mid-market to enterprise corporate
- **Popularity:** Multiple Brandon Hall and G2 awards; growing fast in North America
- **Key Differentiators:** Absorb Infuse (embed learning in any app), eCommerce capabilities, AI-powered admin, modern UX, strong reporting
- **Why Priority:** Rising enterprise player with eCommerce angle relevant to Thinkific comparison.

## Tier 2 — Strong Contenders (High Priority)

### 16. D2L Brightspace
- **Description:** Learning platform serving education and corporate markets. Canadian company (Kitchener, ON). Strong in K-12 and higher ed.
- **Target Audience:** Higher education, K-12, corporate
- **Popularity:** Used by 1,100+ institutions; publicly traded on TSX (DSAI)
- **Key Differentiators:** Adaptive learning, competency-based education support, accessibility-first design, strong analytics
- **Why Priority:** Major education LMS, Canadian company (like Thinkific), growing corporate division.

### 17. 360Learning
- **Description:** Collaborative learning platform that turns internal experts into course creators. Combines LMS with collaborative authoring.
- **Target Audience:** Mid-market to enterprise corporate
- **Popularity:** 1,700+ customers; strong growth in Europe and North America
- **Key Differentiators:** Collaborative/peer learning approach, internal expert-driven content, reaction-based feedback system, strong L&D community
- **Why Priority:** Unique approach; growing brand recognition.

### 18. LearnDash
- **Description:** WordPress LMS plugin — the leading LMS solution for WordPress sites. Acquired by StellarWP/Liquid Web.
- **Target Audience:** WordPress users, SMBs, experts, training companies
- **Popularity:** Powers 80,000+ course sites; #1 WordPress LMS plugin
- **Key Differentiators:** WordPress-native (full control), one-time pricing (no monthly fees on legacy plans), flexible, huge ecosystem of add-ons
- **Why Priority:** Very different technical approach; popular with WordPress users comparing to hosted platforms.

### 19. Coursera for Business / Coursera for Campus
- **Description:** Major MOOC platform offering university-partnered courses. B2B arm (Coursera for Business) serves corporate upskilling.
- **Target Audience:** Consumer learners, enterprise (upskilling), universities (campus)
- **Popularity:** 148M+ registered learners, 7,000+ institutions, publicly traded (COUR)
- **Key Differentiators:** University-branded content, degree programs, professional certificates (Google, IBM, Meta), massive content library
- **Why Priority:** Massive brand; different model but compared in "best learning platforms."

### 20. LinkedIn Learning (formerly Lynda.com)
- **Description:** Professional development platform integrated with LinkedIn. Offers 21,000+ courses taught by industry experts.
- **Target Audience:** Professionals, enterprise L&D teams
- **Popularity:** 27M+ learners; integrated into LinkedIn's 1B+ member network
- **Key Differentiators:** LinkedIn profile integration, skill assessments, AI-driven recommendations, enterprise reporting, massive content library
- **Why Priority:** Huge brand awareness; common comparison for corporate learning buyers.

### 21. Workday Learning
- **Description:** Learning module within the Workday HCM suite. Tightly integrated with Workday's HR, payroll, and talent management.
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise (existing Workday customers)
- **Popularity:** Workday serves 10,000+ customers; Learning is an add-on module
- **Key Differentiators:** Native HCM integration, skills-based learning, embedded in employee workflow, AI-powered (Sana partnership)
- **Why Priority:** Major enterprise player; Workday customers evaluating learning options.

### 22. iSpring Learn
- **Description:** Corporate LMS with best-in-class authoring tool integration. Strong in compliance and structured training.
- **Target Audience:** SMB to mid-market corporate
- **Popularity:** 59,000+ customers; strong presence in compliance-heavy industries
- **Key Differentiators:** Tight integration with iSpring Suite authoring tool, rapid course creation, strong mobile app, on-the-job training tracking
- **Why Priority:** Strong in compliance space; good SMB bridge.

### 23. Systeme.io
- **Description:** All-in-one marketing platform with course hosting, email, funnels, and affiliate management. French-founded, budget-friendly.
- **Target Audience:** Solopreneurs, small creators, budget-conscious experts
- **Popularity:** 500,000+ users; fastest-growing in budget segment
- **Key Differentiators:** Free plan with courses, extremely affordable, all-in-one (funnels + email + courses), simple pricing
- **Why Priority:** Growing rapidly as budget Thinkific/Kajabi alternative.

### 24. Mighty Networks
- **Description:** Community-first platform that combines courses, memberships, and community in one branded space.
- **Target Audience:** Community builders, experts, coaches
- **Popularity:** Strong growth in community-driven learning space
- **Key Differentiators:** Community-first approach, native mobile apps (branded), events, bundles, "People Magic" AI features
- **Why Priority:** Growing competitor in the expert/community learning space.

### 25. Tovuti LMS
- **Description:** Cloud-based LMS focused on interactive content creation and learner engagement. Features built-in authoring with 40+ gamified content types.
- **Target Audience:** Mid-market corporate, training companies
- **Popularity:** Growing; strong G2 ratings
- **Key Differentiators:** Built-in authoring with 40+ content types, interactive video, virtual classroom, eCommerce, white-label
- **Why Priority:** Rising mid-market LMS with strong content creation features.

### 26. Thought Industries
- **Description:** Purpose-built platform for customer and partner education (external training). Strong in B2B SaaS customer education.
- **Target Audience:** B2B companies doing customer/partner education
- **Popularity:** Leader in customer education category; used by major SaaS companies
- **Key Differentiators:** External training focus, multi-tenant, commerce engine, content authoring, learning analytics tied to business outcomes
- **Why Priority:** Directly relevant to Thinkific's "Companies" buyer persona (customer training).

### 27. Skilljar
- **Description:** Customer education platform for B2B companies. Focused on onboarding, training, and certifying customers and partners.
- **Target Audience:** B2B SaaS, technology companies (customer/partner education)
- **Popularity:** Used by Cisco, Zendesk, Verizon, and 100s of B2B companies
- **Key Differentiators:** Purpose-built for external/customer education, native integrations with CRMs and CSPs, certification management, analytics
- **Why Priority:** Key competitor for Thinkific's customer training use case.

### 28. Open edX
- **Description:** Open-source learning platform powering edX.org (now owned by 2U). Used by Harvard, MIT, and governments worldwide.
- **Target Audience:** Universities, governments, large enterprises
- **Popularity:** 100M+ learners reached; used by 2,200+ organizations
- **Key Differentiators:** Open-source (free), scalable to millions, XBlock extensibility, strong in MOOCs, self-paced and instructor-led
- **Why Priority:** Major open-source alternative; relevant for education and enterprise.

## Tier 3 — Long-Tail SEO Opportunities

### 29. Gumroad
- **Description:** Simple platform for selling digital products, including courses. Known for extreme simplicity and creator-friendly terms.
- **Target Audience:** Independent creators, digital product sellers
- **Popularity:** $500M+ in creator sales processed
- **Key Differentiators:** Extreme simplicity, flat fee model, no monthly costs (just transaction fees), supports all digital product types
- **Why Priority:** Often compared to course platforms despite being a digital product platform.

### 30. Lessonly (by Seismic)
- **Description:** Training and enablement platform focused on sales and customer-facing teams. Acquired by Seismic in 2021.
- **Target Audience:** Sales teams, customer service teams, enterprise
- **Popularity:** 4M+ learners; now part of Seismic Learning
- **Key Differentiators:** Practice-based learning, sales enablement integration, rapid lesson creation, coaching tools
- **Why Priority:** Relevant for sales training buyers; "Lessonly" still searched.

### 31. Paylocity LMS
- **Description:** LMS module built into Paylocity's HR and payroll platform. Designed for employee development within existing HR workflow.
- **Target Audience:** SMB to mid-market (existing Paylocity customers)
- **Popularity:** Paylocity serves 36,000+ customers
- **Key Differentiators:** Native HR/payroll integration, compliance tracking, community collaboration, mobile-first
- **Why Priority:** Growing LMS within popular HR platform.

### 32. EdApp (by SafetyCulture)
- **Description:** Mobile-first microlearning LMS. Focuses on bite-sized, gamified training for frontline workers.
- **Target Audience:** Frontline workers, retail, hospitality, manufacturing
- **Popularity:** 200+ countries; acquired by SafetyCulture
- **Key Differentiators:** Microlearning focus, free plan, gamification, AI course creation, mobile-first design, built-in course library
- **Why Priority:** Growing in mobile/frontline training niche.

### 33. Trainual
- **Description:** Business playbook and training platform for documenting processes and onboarding employees. Not a traditional LMS.
- **Target Audience:** SMBs, operations teams
- **Popularity:** 10,000+ SMB customers
- **Key Differentiators:** Process documentation + training combined, role-based content, organizational knowledge base, simple onboarding
- **Why Priority:** Different angle but shows up in LMS searches for SMBs.

### 34. LifterLMS
- **Description:** WordPress LMS plugin offering courses, memberships, and community features. Open-source core with premium add-ons.
- **Target Audience:** WordPress users, small training businesses
- **Popularity:** Second most popular WordPress LMS after LearnDash
- **Key Differentiators:** WordPress-native, open-source core, flexible add-on pricing, community features, eCommerce built-in
- **Why Priority:** WordPress alternative to LearnDash; relevant for WP users.

### 35. Google Classroom
- **Description:** Free LMS by Google for schools. Integrates tightly with Google Workspace (Docs, Drive, Meet).
- **Target Audience:** K-12 education
- **Popularity:** 150M+ users globally; dominant in K-12
- **Key Differentiators:** Free, Google Workspace integration, simple, widely adopted in schools, Chromebook-friendly
- **Why Priority:** Massive user base; relevant for education-focused comparisons.

### 36. Schoology (by PowerSchool)
- **Description:** K-12 and higher ed LMS now part of PowerSchool. Known for standards-aligned assessment and parent engagement.
- **Target Audience:** K-12, some higher education
- **Popularity:** Used by 60,000+ schools
- **Key Differentiators:** Standards alignment, parent/guardian access, gradebook integration, assessment tools, PowerSchool ecosystem
- **Why Priority:** Major K-12 LMS; long-tail SEO value.

### 37. Chamilo
- **Description:** Open-source LMS popular in Latin America, Europe, and developing countries. Simple and lightweight.
- **Target Audience:** Education institutions, NGOs, developing markets
- **Popularity:** Used in 180+ countries; strong in Spanish/French-speaking markets
- **Key Differentiators:** Free/open-source, lightweight, multi-language, low resource requirements, active community
- **Why Priority:** Long-tail SEO for open-source LMS searches.

### 38. Talent Cards (by Epignosis)
- **Description:** Mobile microlearning app by the same company as TalentLMS. Delivers training via card-based mobile content.
- **Target Audience:** Frontline/deskless workers
- **Popularity:** Niche but growing; part of Epignosis ecosystem
- **Key Differentiators:** Card-based microlearning, mobile-only, push notifications, offline access
- **Why Priority:** Niche but pairs with TalentLMS article for comprehensive coverage.

### 39. Docebo Shape / Docebo Content
- **Description:** AI-powered content creation and curation tool within the Docebo ecosystem. Auto-generates microlearning from existing materials.
- **Target Audience:** Enterprise L&D teams
- **Popularity:** Part of Docebo's growing AI suite
- **Key Differentiators:** AI content generation, auto-curation from existing docs/videos, microlearning creation
- **Why Priority:** Supports Docebo article; shows AI trend in LMS.

### 40. Sakai
- **Description:** Open-source LMS for higher education. Community-developed, used by research universities.
- **Target Audience:** Higher education, research universities
- **Popularity:** Used by Duke, NYU, and other research universities; declining market share
- **Key Differentiators:** Open-source, strong assessment tools, academic focus, community-governed
- **Why Priority:** Long-tail for open-source education LMS searches.

## Notes for Article Writing

1. **Article Format:** Each article will be "Thinkific vs [Platform]" — a comparison guide optimized for SEO.
2. **Saba Note:** Saba was acquired by Cornerstone in 2020 and is being migrated to "Cornerstone Galaxy." Write a standalone Saba article (people still search for it) but note the Cornerstone connection heavily.
3. **Cornerstone Note:** Cover both legacy Cornerstone LMS and the new Cornerstone Galaxy platform. Mention Saba and EdCast acquisitions.
4. **Litmos Note:** SAP sold Litmos to Francisco Partners in 2022. Now just "Litmos" but "SAP Litmos" is still the dominant search term.
5. **Buyer Personas:** Each article should speak to the relevant persona — Experts (creator platforms), Academies (education LMS), Companies (enterprise/customer education).
6. **Content Strategy:** Tier 1 articles should be 2,500–3,500 words. Tier 2 can be 1,800–2,500. Tier 3 can be 1,200–1,800.
